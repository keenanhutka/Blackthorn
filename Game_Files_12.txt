RACES_REFERENCE - Version 1.6 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
The Shattered Principalities are home to eight primary sentient races, each shaped by the Sundering War and the 46 AE famine. Seventy years later, racial relations remain a volatile mix of cosmopolitan mixing in cities like Thornhaven and deep-seated animosity in backwards enclaves. Slavery and erotic power dynamics affect every race differently — some embrace ownership as natural hierarchy, others resent it as a tool of subjugation, and a few have developed unique cultural adaptations.
Body traits (see Body_Ranges_Reference) vary significantly by race and heavily influence slave market value, BDSM compatibility, crafting of custom restraints or pleasure items, and social perception. Races with naturally high Attractiveness or favorable genital ranges command premium prices, while those with strong natural scent or hygiene challenges require more maintenance.
Sexual Corruption (0–100) is a tracked stat that reflects how “broken” or “trained” an NPC has become through sexual acts, BDSM, and mental manipulation. It allows highly corrupt NPCs to safely accommodate larger sizes without magic or alchemy and changes how they respond to depraved acts (pure NPCs dislike anything beyond vanilla; highly corrupt NPCs crave extreme play).
All races can interbreed to varying degrees of success, though offspring often inherit mixed traits and face social prejudice. Magic and alchemical alteration can blur racial lines further, usually at great personal risk.

RACIAL MATURITY & THE ADULT TRAIT
All sex scenes, BDSM mechanics, breeding rules, and related content in the Sex_BDSM_System.txt, Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules.txt, Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt, and any other file are strictly limited to adult characters and NPCs only.
- “Adult” is a binary trait that must be present on a character sheet or NPC profile before any erotic or breeding mechanics can be applied.
- Maturity age (when the Adult trait is gained) varies by race due to differing lifespans and biological development:
  - Goblins: Mature fastest — reach adulthood at 12–14 years.
  - Orcs and Dryads: Next tier — reach adulthood at 16–18 years.
  - Humans, Gnomes, and Giants: Standard maturation — reach adulthood at 18–20 years.
  - Elves and Dwarves: Mature slowest (due to long lifespans) — reach adulthood at 25–30 years (elves) or 30–40 years (dwarves).
- Any character or NPC below their race’s maturity age automatically lacks the “Adult” trait and cannot participate in any sex, BDSM, breeding, or related scenes. Attempts to involve non-adult individuals automatically fail, trigger severe narrative and mechanical consequences (including massive Defiance spikes, faction scandals, church inquisitions, legal penalties under Crime_Justice_System, or campaign-ending repercussions depending on table tone), and are strictly prohibited.
- This rule is a hard prerequisite across the entire system. The “Adult” trait is tracked alongside other Body traits and serves as the mechanical gate ensuring all erotic content remains adult-only.
- When creating or advancing NPCs or player characters, assign the Adult trait once the character reaches their race’s maturity age. Offspring from breeding scenes automatically begin without the Adult trait and gain it only upon reaching racial maturity.

RANDOM_EVENTS_TABLE - Version 1.15 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
Random events drive the volatile, consequence-heavy nature of the Shattered Principalities. Travel, district exploration, daily estate routines, and life in the five baronies are never safe or predictable. Events tie directly into Defiance growth, faction reputation, property damage, black market detection, magical backlash, economic fluctuations, and generational consequences. Every roll should feel grimdark: even “positive” outcomes carry risk, cost, or moral compromise. With Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life now fully integrated, events frequently trigger during Labor, Maintenance, Oversight, or Recovery Blocks, amplifying the daily burden of ownership.
How to Use These Tables
Roll once per major travel leg, per full day in a settlement district, once per weekly estate review, or when the GM deems appropriate (especially after Labor Block, festival participation, or aging checks).
Add modifiers based on context (high-value cargo, high-Defiance slaves, magical vehicles, faction reputation, current estate maintenance quality, owner aging, etc.).
Many events link to other systems (Vehicle_System travel risks, Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life routines, Crime_Justice_System detection, Factions_and_Politics reputation shifts, Magic_System backlash, Property_Ownership damage, Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics rebellion chains, etc.).
Events are modular: use the general Travel table for journeys, Settlement/District or Barony-Specific tables for urban play, and Estate-Specific complications during daily routines.
Player Reputation Modifiers
These optional modifiers personalize event frequency and severity based on the player’s current Social Standing Tier (from Social_Advancement.txt) and Faction Reputation scores.
Social Standing Tier Modifiers (apply to all event rolls)

Tier 0 (Street Level): No modifier. Events feel raw and dangerous.
Tier 1–2 (Rising): –1 to all event rolls (slightly fewer catastrophic events) but +2 to severity of negative outcomes if they occur (reputation matters more now).
Tier 3–4 (Established): +1 to all event rolls when in public or noble districts (opportunities increase) but –2 to event rolls in slums or black-market areas (you stand out more). Negative events involving scandal or Defiance carry double reputation bleed.
Tier 5 (Powerhouse): +2 to all event rolls in high-society or faction-heavy areas (you attract major attention) but –3 in low districts (you are seen as an outsider or target). Major positive events can grant permanent faction boons; major negative events can cause estate-wide crises.
Faction Reputation Modifiers (apply when relevant to the faction or barony)
High Positive Reputation (+20 or higher with a faction): +2 to rolls involving that faction’s events (more beneficial offers) but –2 to rolls involving rival factions (increased hostility).
Low or Negative Reputation (–10 or worse): –2 to rolls involving that faction (fewer opportunities) but +2 to severity of backlash if caught.
Example: High Silken Collar reputation makes Silken Collar Grand Revel events more frequent and rewarding but makes Broken Chain events harsher.
Owner aging or visible decline from Time_System.txt v1.1 can further modify severity (e.g., +2 to negative event outcomes or +1 to Defiance spikes if the owner appears weakened).
