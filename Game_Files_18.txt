WILDERNESS_AREAS - Version 1.16 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
The wilderness surrounding and between the five baronies is a lawless, dangerous expanse of dense forests, rugged hills, thorny badlands, and hidden coves. Seventy years after the Sundering and the 46 AE famine, these areas are filled with ancient ruins, cursed battlefields, independent racial enclaves, Freeborn League safehouses, and pockets of raw magical instability. Travel and resource gathering here offer high rewards (rare reagents, unclaimed land for Property_Ownership, escaped slaves, black-market caches) but carry constant grimdark risk: ambush, Defiance spikes, magical backlash, disease, starvation, and moral compromise. The wilderness is where the true cost of empire-building is felt — and where ambitious players can carve out new holdings, raid rivals, or forge dangerous alliances.
How to Use These Tables
Roll once per day of wilderness travel/exploration or when the GM deems appropriate.
Add modifiers from Random_Events_Table seasonal and lunar cycle modifiers, Vehicle_System type, current faction reputation, number of slaves, and cargo value.
Many events link directly to other systems (Random_Events_Table, Magic_System backlash, Crime_Justice_System pursuit, Factions_and_Politics reputation, Property_Ownership claims, Defiance growth, etc.).
Events are modular and can chain together for narrative depth.
General Wilderness Encounter Table (d20)
1–2 Harsh Terrain or Weather: Progress slowed or halted. +5–15 Defiance to slaves; Hygiene/Scent degradation. Possible minor property/vehicle damage.
3–4 Ambush by Bandits or Raiders: Combat or negotiation opportunity. Ties to Crime_Justice_System and Factions_and_Politics.
5–6 Freeborn League Contact or Ambush: Sympathetic cell offers aid or attempts rescue of slaves. Major reputation shifts.
7–8 Resource Opportunity: Rare herbs, ore, or ancient ruins. Roll on Alchemy_Consumables_and_Potions or Raw_Materials_Tools_Trade_Goods for finds.
9–10 Slave Escape Attempt or Defiance Spike: High-Defiance slaves revolt or flee. Requires immediate BDSM motivation or force.
11–12 Magical Anomaly or Ruin: Backlash roll on Magic_System. Possible enchanted item or cursed artifact.
13–14 Disease or Contamination Outbreak: Poor Hygiene/Scent conditions spread illness. Affects slaves and player characters.
15–16 Independent Racial Enclave: Orc hold, dryad grove, or giant camp. Diplomacy, trade, or conflict opportunity.
17–18 Hidden Cache or Smuggler’s Den: Black-market goods or escaped slaves. Ties to Economy_Trade_System and Shadow Forge.
19–20 Major Threat or Windfall: Monster attack, ancient guardian, or valuable unclaimed land suitable for Property_Ownership claim.

- Daily Routine Framework and Seasonal Event Tables from Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life modify wilderness travel: well-managed estates with loyal overseers and active security patrols grant –3 to –8 on encounter rolls or reduce Defiance spikes from harsh terrain.
- Overseer NPC Mechanics and loyalty tracking affect expeditions launched from player properties. Loyal overseers improve resource gathering efficiency and reduce sabotage risk during wilderness labor; betrayal events can turn a routine gathering trip into a Defiance spike or ambush trigger.
- Estate Security Patrols and Beast Security Mechanics provide direct bonuses to wilderness survival: beast patrols reduce ambush chances near owned land, while failed security can escalate a minor encounter into a raid that damages Property_Ownership holdings.
- Resource & Industrial properties gain the strongest synergy. On-site estate routines (Labor Block with proper maintenance and Interaction Block motivation scenes) amplify the listed on-site crafting bonuses and stabilize Defiance growth during wilderness-adjacent work.
- Seasonal modifiers now apply across both systems: Autumn harvest boosts from estates can coincide with rich wilderness foraging, while Winter hardship increases Defiance drift and encounter severity in border zones.

Specific Interactions:
- Slavery & Defiance: Harsh wilderness conditions amplify daily Defiance drift from Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life. BDSM motivation scenes become critical during expeditions; poor estate security can turn a single escape probe into a full rebellion chain.
- Property_Ownership: Successful wilderness exploration can lead to new claims (mines, lumber camps, hunting cabins) that then require full estate management routines to remain profitable and secure.
- Economy_Trade_System: Resource gathering in the wilderness directly feeds market fluctuations and raw material supply. Well-managed estates with strong security reduce smuggling or caravan losses during wilderness legs.
- Vehicle_System: Wilderness travel amplifies breakdown and slave-draught risks; estate-managed luxury stables or vehicle sheds (with beast security) provide repair bonuses and safer staging points.
- Factions_and_Politics & Crime_Justice_System: Encounters with racial enclaves or Freeborn League cells cause stronger reputation shifts when the player has visible estate holdings nearby. Failed estate security can escalate wilderness crimes into barony-wide investigations.

Racial Enclave Cultures (Detailed – 70 AE)
Independent racial enclaves are scattered throughout the wilderness and border zones. These self-governing communities maintain wary autonomy from human baronies. Each has its own grimdark culture, attitudes toward slavery, erotic power dynamics, and interactions with outsiders. Encounters with these enclaves often lead to diplomacy, trade, conflict, or uneasy alliances that can dramatically shift reputation and open new Property_Ownership or quest opportunities.
Orc Holds
Orc enclaves are militaristic strongholds built into rocky hills or thorny badlands (most common in Bloodthorn and Ironvein border zones). Society is clan-based, honor-driven, and brutally hierarchical. Slavery exists but is usually temporary — captured enemies are worked hard, then either ransomed, adopted, or executed in ritual combat. Erotic culture emphasizes raw physical dominance and breeding strength; high Penile/Anal/Flagellation skills are prized. Orcs view human-style multi-generational slavery as weak and decadent.
Mechanical Ties: High natural bonuses to Endurance, Flagellation, and Unarmed Combat. Defiance growth is lower for orc slaves under harsh but “honorable” treatment.
Player Hooks: Raid alliances, gladiatorial challenges, or breeding contracts for strong offspring.
Reputation Effects: Friendly orcs grant combat bonuses and safe passage; hostility leads to brutal ambushes.
Orc Honor Duels (Expanded)
Orc society resolves disputes, leadership challenges, and even trade negotiations through ritualized Honor Duels. These are public, brutal, and frequently erotic displays of dominance. A duel is declared when honor is slighted (theft of crafted goods, broken alliance, poor treatment of an orc slave, or disrespect of strength).
Duel Levels & Mechanical Effects
Minor Duel (disrespect or small slight): Unarmed Combat or Grappling with BDSM elements (restraint, flagellation). Loser suffers +20 temporary Defiance and –5 to social rolls for 1 month.
Moderate Duel (broken contract or raid interference): Full combat with weapons, often ending in public submission or breeding claim. Loser’s faction reputation drops –15 with all orc clans.
Blood Duel (major betrayal or killing of kin): Lethal or near-lethal combat. Winner may claim the loser (or their slaves) as property. Permanent –30 Reputation with the clan and possible Blood Grudge bleed.
Erotic & Mechanical Rules
Duels always incorporate high Penile/Anal/Flagellation and Unarmed Combat. The dominant partner gains Pleasure Point bonuses and can force temporary enslavement or breeding rights on the loser.
High Sexual Corruption orcs treat the duel as a dominance ritual and may gain minor PP or kink discovery even in defeat.
Refusing a duel is considered cowardice and causes immediate –40 Reputation with all orc enclaves.
Resolution & Player Hooks
Win a duel to gain safe passage, trade bonuses, or temporary orc allies.
Lose a duel and negotiate terms (e.g., temporary labor or BDSM “reparations”) to reduce severity.
Use a duel to settle a larger conflict or claim valuable land/resources.
Reputation Effects
Respectful participation in duels grants +10 to +30 Reputation with orc clans and related factions (e.g., House Thorne, Crimson Watch, Bloodvein).
Dishonorable behavior (cheating, using magic, or poor sportsmanship) triggers Blood Grudge-level enmity across all orc enclaves.

MEDICAL_TRAUMA - Version 1.0 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE - Shattered Principalities)

Core Philosophy
In the Shattered Principalities, the body is a commodity that breaks, festers, and deteriorates under the twin weights of exploitation and time. Seventy years after the Sundering and the 46 AE famine, medical knowledge is a patchwork of herbal tradition, brutal butchery, and dangerous alchemical experimentation. Healing is never clean—surgery leaves scars that lower market value, plagues spread through crowded slave quarters, and the mind fractures under cumulative trauma.

This system complements Time_System.txt v1.1 (aging), Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt v1.8 (Defiance spikes from suffering), and Alchemy_Consumables_and_Potions.txt v1.5 (treatments). It provides mechanical consequences for violence, neglect, and magical corruption beyond simple HP loss.

TRAUMA & INJURY SYSTEM
================================================================
Injury Severity Levels
Beyond HP damage, injuries cause permanent or semi-permanent Body trait modifications, skill penalties, and Defiance spikes.

Minor Injuries (DC 12–15 to treat)
- Cuts, bruises, minor burns, sprains
- Mechanical Effects: –1 to physical skill checks for 1–3 days; +2 daily Defiance for slaves (discomfort)
- Permanent Marks: 10% chance of faint scarring (–1 to Attractiveness if facial)

Moderate Injuries (DC 16–22 to treat)
- Deep lacerations, broken bones, dislocated joints, severe burns
- Mechanical Effects: –3 to related physical skills for 1–2 weeks; –5 to Defiance management rolls; +5 daily Defiance
- Permanent Marks: 40% chance of noticeable scarring (–2 to –5 Attractiveness), limited mobility (–1 to Dexterity)

Major Injuries (DC 23–28 to treat)
- Amputations (fingers/toes), crushed limbs, gouged eyes, torn genitalia (from size-mismatch trauma)
- Mechanical Effects: Permanent –5 to –15 in related Body traits; cannot perform specific labor; +10–20 daily Defiance; possible Rebellion Chain trigger
- Permanent Marks: Obvious disfigurement (–10 to –25 Attractiveness), missing limbs require prosthetics or magical replacement (backlash risk)

Catastrophic Injuries (DC 29+ to treat, often fatal)
- Severed limbs, shattered pelvis, internal bleeding, brain damage
- Mechanical Effects: Permanent disability or death; survivors gain "Broken" trait (+20 Defiance floor, cannot perform labor)
- Permanent Marks: Massive disfigurement (–30+ Attractiveness), chronic pain conditions (monthly Con saves vs. incapacitation)

Infection Risk
Untreated wounds (longer than 24 hours without proper care) risk infection:
- Roll d20 + (Hygiene score ÷ 10) vs. DC 15
- Failure: Develops into Moderate Disease (see below) within 2–5 days
- Critical Failure (natural 1): Rapid gangrene or septic shock; death within 24 hours without amputation or magical intervention

DISEASE & PLAGUE SYSTEM

The 46 AE famine left lasting biological scars. Crowded estates, poor Hygiene, and necromantic "death scent" create perfect plague vectors.

Disease Severity & Mechanics
Diseases progress through Stages, each requiring Con saves (d20 + Con modifier + Medicine skill if treated) vs. Disease DC.

- No symptoms; contagious if airborne/contact
- Detection: Medicine/Insight DC 18 to identify

Stage 2: Symptoms (3–7 days)
- –2 to all physical stats; +5 daily Defiance (misery)
- Visible signs: Rashes, cough, fever (scandal risk if noble)

Stage 3: Critical (7–14 days)
- –5 to all stats; incapacitated without bed rest
- Permanent damage risk: Failed save causes –1 to random Body trait
- Death risk: Failed Con save by 5+ = death

Stage 4: Recovery/Death
- Successful treatment: 1–4 weeks recovery; possible permanent –1 to Constitution
- Failure: Death or chronic carrier status (spreads to others)
