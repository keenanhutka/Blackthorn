BESTIARY_SHATTERED_PRINCIPALITIES - Version 1.0 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)

Core Philosophy
The wilderness between the five baronies teems with horrors born from the Sundering War's magical fallout, necromantic experiments gone wrong, and planar entities that slipped through rifts during the cataclysm. Seventy years later, these creatures have infested ancient ruins, polluted industrial zones, and the dreams of the corrupted. 

Unlike standard beasts, many require the specific mechanics of the Shattered Principalities to harm or bind—Sundering Horrors carry mutations that interact with Body trait systems, Planar Entities demand BDSM-style binding rituals to control, and Undead variants feed on Defiance and Sexual Corruption. This bestiary integrates directly with Combat_Rules v1.7 (grapple/erotic combat), Magic_System v1.13 (binding rituals), and BDSM_Restraints_and_Pleasure_Gear.txt (specialized containment).

INTERROGATION_INFORMATION_EXTRACTION - Version 1.0 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE - Shattered Principalities)

Core Philosophy
Information is the true currency of power in the Shattered Principalities—more valuable than gold or flesh. Seventy years of factional intrigue, black market dealings, and noble house conspiracies have refined interrogation into a dark art that blurs the line between pleasure and pain, compliance and betrayal. 

Unlike standard BDSM motivation scenes (which reduce Defiance to increase productivity), interrogation targets the mind’s resistance to revealing secrets. It employs distinct mechanics: Willpower vs. Pain tolerance, information reliability checks, and the risk of creating damaged but useful double agents. This system integrates with Medical_Trauma.txt (psychological damage), Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt (breaking points), and Crime_Justice_System.txt (legal testimony).

================================================================
CORE MECHANICS: THE INTERROGATION CYCLE
================================================================
Every interrogation follows a three-phase cycle: Approach, Pressure, and Verification. Unlike combat or erotic scenes, the goal is not submission in the physical sense, but cognitive surrender—the breaking of mental walls while keeping the subject alive and potentially useful.

Phase 1: Approach (Intelligence Gathering)
Before physical contact, determine the subject’s psychological profile:
- Primary Motivation: Roll Insight DC 15 or use Espionage skill to identify what the subject values most (family safety, physical comfort, pride, religious belief, fear of specific traumas)
- Resistance Threshold: Calculate base Willpower (d20 + Wis modifier + relevant background bonuses)
- Information Value: Determine what they know (GM assigns tiers: Minor gossip, Faction secrets, Critical blackmail, Strategic intelligence)

Phase 2: Pressure (The Breaking)
Choose methodology and engage in contested rolls:

Physical Torture (Intimidation + Medicine)
- Mechanics: d20 + Intimidation skill + equipment bonuses vs. subject’s Willpower + Constitution modifier
- Duration: 10 minutes per "session"
- Effects: On success, subject reveals information but gains 1d6 Insanity Points (Medical_Trauma.txt) and permanent +5 Defiance floor (hates interrogator)
- Critical Success (beat by 10+): Subject breaks completely, reveals everything including hidden information, but suffers Major Psychological Trauma (permanent –2 to Wisdom, gains phobia of interrogator)
- Failure: Subject resists, gains +10 temporary Willpower against future attempts for 24 hours, may lie (see False Confessions below)

Pleasure Interrogation (Seduction + BDSM skills)
- Mechanics: d20 + Seduction + Erotic Performance vs. subject’s Willpower + Sexual Corruption modifier (high Corruption makes this harder—they enjoy it too much)
- Duration: 30–60 minutes per session
- Effects: On success, subject reveals information while gaining +5 Sexual Corruption (becomes pliant to interrogator specifically, –10 Defiance toward them only)
- Critical Success: Subject becomes obsessively loyal to interrogator (Stockholm Syndrome), reveals everything, and can be turned as a double agent
- Failure: Subject recognizes manipulation, gains +15 Defiance, becomes immune to Seduction from that interrogator permanently

Psychological Manipulation (Deception + Insight)
- Mechanics: d20 + Deception vs. subject’s Insight DC
- Method: False flag operations, convincing subject their faction abandoned them, "good cop" routine
- Duration: Hours to days (requires multiple scenes)
- Effects: Success reduces Willpower by 5 for future physical interrogation attempts; no physical trauma but requires high Intelligence (subject may outwit interrogator)
- Critical Failure: Subject turns tables, feeds false intel to interrogator (see Counter-Espionage)

Phase 3: Verification (The Problem of Lies)
Information extracted under duress is unreliable. Roll Intelligence (Investigation) or Insight vs. DC set by subject’s Deception skill:
- Success: Information verified as true (or identifies specific falsehoods)
- Failure: Cannot determine truth; 50% chance information contains critical falsehood that endangers operations
- Critical Success: Identifies not just truth, but what subject withheld (they reveal 80% but hide the crucial 20%)

================================================================
RESISTANCE & BREAKING MECHANICS
================================================================
Willpower Thresholds
Unlike Defiance (which ranges 0–100), Willpower represents immediate mental resistance during interrogation:

Willpower 1–5 (Broken): Already cracked, reveals information on any successful roll. Common in subjects who have already suffered Major Trauma.

Willpower 6–12 (Vulnerable): Standard captive. Can be broken with 2–3 successful sessions.

Willpower 13–20 (Trained): Agents, fanatics, or high-level faction members. Require specialized methods or 5+ sessions over days/weeks.

Willpower 21+ (Fanatical): Broken Chain martyrs, Silken Collar inquisitors, House Whisperveil agents. May require magical interrogation or months of psychological manipulation.

Breaking Points
Each time a subject fails a Willpower save by 5+, they suffer a Breaking Point:
- Immediate: Reveals current information tier
- Permanent: –2 to future Willpower, gain 1d4 Insanity Points
- Three Breaking Points: Subject becomes "Hollowed"—permanently compliant but useless for complex tasks (automation only, cannot be a double agent)

False Confessions & Fabrication
Under extreme physical torture (failed by 5+ on Verification check), subjects will invent information to stop pain:
- Roll Deception (subject) vs. Insight (interrogator)
- If subject wins: Feeds plausible but false intel that sends interrogators into ambushes or wastes resources
- Detection: Requires external verification (days/weeks of investigation)

================================================================
Successfully turned subjects become assets. Requires:
1. Breaking (reducing Willpower to 5 or below via any method)

- Subject gains "Turned" trait: +10 Defiance toward former allies, –20 Defiance toward handler
- Weekly loyalty check: d20 vs. DC 15; failure indicates relapse or secret communication with old faction
- If caught communicating: Immediate Rebellion Chain activation (subject may attack handler or flee)

Long-term Maintenance:
- Must provide regular "rewards" (BDSM scenes, luxuries, or genuine affection) or Willpower recovers and subject may turn again
- Risk: Turned agents often develop genuine affection mixed with hatred, creating dangerous emotional volatility
