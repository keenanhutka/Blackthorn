LEGACY_TRACKER - Version 1.3 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE - Shattered Principalities)

Core Philosophy
This appendix provides a simple, practical tracking tool for long-term generational play in the Shattered Principalities. It works alongside Time_System.txt v1.1 (Monthly Aging Checks, seasonal effects, longevity potions/rituals), Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules.txt v1.11 (bloodline creation, hybrid traits, maturation), NPC_System.txt v1.15 (Body traits, Defiance, Sexual Corruption for offspring), Social_Advancement.txt v1.5 (legacy benefits at Tier 5), CAMPAIGN_MILESTONE_GUIDE.txt v1.5 (Phase 4 generational hand-off), and ADVANCED_ADVENTURES.txt v1.1 (late-phase scenarios involving succession and bloodline crises).
The tracker emphasizes grimdark reality: bloodlines are valuable assets but also sources of risk. Offspring inherit mixed traits, Defiance can compound across generations, aging affects founder and descendants differently, and legacy is never guaranteed — rebellion, scandal, or poor longevity management can erase decades of work. Use this sheet (or digital equivalent) to track founder decline, maturing offspring, and multi-generational estates without slowing down play.

How to Use the Legacy Tracker

Update once per in-game month or after major Time_System checkpoints (breeding, aging rolls, festivals, rebellions).
Track the founder (PC) separately from bloodline offspring.
Roll or assign inherited traits using Body_Ranges_Reference, Random_NPC_Generator v1.3 (race/faction variants), and Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules hybrid rules.
Reference Alchemy_Consumables_and_Potions v1.5 and Magic_System v1.12 for longevity treatments and their backlash.
Visible founder decline (low Attractiveness/Endurance from aging) can trigger Random_Events_Table modifiers or Social_Advancement reputation penalties.

Founder Retirement & Succession Rules (New)
When the founder’s visible decline becomes severe (e.g., cumulative –5 or more to Attractiveness/Endurance or multiple failed longevity treatments), the following options become available:

Voluntary Retirement: Founder steps back to “advisory” role. Grants +1 permanent Social Advancement bonus to chosen heir but risks +10–20 Defiance drift among older slaves who respected the founder personally.
Forced Succession: High-Defiance offspring or faction pressure forces hand-off. Requires a major BDSM/public display scene or Legacy_Tracker roll; failure can trigger rebellion or reputation loss.
Shared Rule: Founder and heir co-manage for 1–2 years. Reduces immediate penalties but increases internal faction intrigue risk (ties to ADVANCED_ADVENTURES Phase 4 scenarios).
Dramatic Exit: Founder makes one final legendary act (grand festival, heroic stand, or ritual sacrifice). Grants massive one-time Social Advancement XP but permanently removes founder from play.

Inheritance Probability Tables (New)
Use these quick guidelines when generating offspring (roll d10 or choose for narrative):
1–3: Strong inheritance from mother (dominant race traits + 60% chance of her Body highlights)
4–6: Blended hybrid (average of both parents + possible random mutation from Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules)
7–8: Strong inheritance from father (dominant race traits + 60% chance of his Body highlights)
9–10: Unexpected outcome (roll on hybrid/mutation table or apply faction influence if relevant)

Bloodline Creation Rules
Bloodlines are created through successful pregnancies (Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules v1.11). Each offspring receives:

Base Race: Determined by mother (primary) with possible hybrid influence from father.
Trait Inheritance: Roll once on the table below for each major Body trait category (Height, Weight, General Attractiveness, Breast Size, Buttocks Size, Genital Scale, Foot Attractiveness, Scent, Hygiene baseline, Sexual Corruption starting value).
Bloodline Trait Inheritance Table (d100, using Body_Ranges_Reference ranges)
01-40: Strong Maternal Inheritance – Use mother’s race range fully; add +10 to any one trait the mother excels in (e.g., high Scent for dryads).
41-70: Blended Hybrid – Average both parents’ racial ranges; roll d20 modifier (±10) on 1–2 key traits for unique expression.
71-85: Strong Paternal Inheritance – Use father’s race range fully; add +10 to any one trait the father excels in.
86-95: Unexpected Mutation – Roll on Body_Ranges_Reference with +15 or –15 random swing; possible alchemical/magical influence (Alchemy_Consumables_and_Potions or Magic_System backlash).
96-100: Signature Bloodline Manifestation – Stabilize one permanent bonus trait for the entire bloodline (see Signature Bloodline Bonuses/Curses below). All future offspring in this line gain +5 to that trait.
Generational Aging Modifiers

Founder: Full Time_System v1.1 Monthly Aging Checks apply. Cumulative decline reduces social rolls (–1 per –5 Attractiveness/Endurance lost).
First Generation Offspring: Aging severity reduced by 20% until they reach maturity. After maturity, normal aging but +1 to Monthly Aging Checks if founder is still alive and providing guidance.
Second+ Generations: Aging severity reduced by 10% per stabilized signature trait. Failed longevity treatments on founder can pass “Weak Blood” curse (–10% fertility and +5 starting Defiance floor for all descendants).
Longevity Potion Backlash: Any backlash on founder (e.g., sudden 10–20 year visible aging) applies half severity to all living bloodline members as “Inherited Decay” (–2 to Attractiveness/Endurance, +10 Defiance floor).
Founder Decline Mechanics
Visible Decline Thresholds (cumulative from aging or backlash):

Mild (–1 to –3 Attractiveness/Endurance): –1 to seduction/domination rolls; minor reputation dip with decadent factions.
Moderate (–4 to –7): –2 to social rolls; slaves gain +5 daily Defiance drift when interacting with founder; possible Random_Events complications.
Severe (–8 or worse): –4 to social rolls; high risk of Forced Succession; founder treated as “has-been” by factions; +15–30 Defiance floor for all estate slaves who witness public decline.
Founder can mitigate with luxury fashion/footwear maintenance (LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS) or alchemical treatments, but neglect accelerates decline.

Roll or choose when a Signature Manifestation occurs (d10):
Bonuses (positive stabilization):
1–2: Alluring Scent – All bloodline members gain permanent +10 Scent; +2 to seduction and aftercare rolls.

Curses (grimdark risk on failed stabilization or backlash):
1–2: Frail Blood – –10 Endurance; aging severity increased by 20% for all descendants.
3–4: Defiant Echo – All offspring start with +15 Defiance floor; higher rebellion risk.
5–6: Scent of Decay – Permanent –10 Scent; Hygiene degrades twice as fast; –2 to market value.
7–8: Barren Shadow – –30% fertility; 25% chance of stillbirth or mutation per pregnancy.
9–10: Marked Decline – Visible aging appears 5–10 years early; founder and all descendants suffer –3 to social rolls in public.

Succession Crises
When succession becomes necessary, roll or choose crisis type (d8) and resolve with Legacy_Tracker roll or scene:
1–2: Heir Rebellion – High-Defiance offspring challenges founder publicly (requires BDSM domination or combat scene).
3–4: Faction Interference – External faction demands specific heir or threatens to back rival (ties to Factions_and_Politics).
5–6: Bloodline Split – Multiple viable heirs create internal factions; risk of estate division or civil war.
7–8: Legacy Fracture – Founder’s decline causes mass Defiance spike; must perform public hand-off ceremony or lose 20–40% income and reputation.

LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS - Version 1.2 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE - Shattered Principalities)

Core Philosophy
In the Shattered Principalities, clothing and accessories are never mere decoration. They are public declarations of power, wealth, erotic mastery, and social standing. A perfectly tailored silk collar, a pair of enchanted heels that force perfect posture, or a revealing gown that highlights flawless Body traits can elevate a lowborn owner faster than gold alone. However, poor maintenance, mismatched sizing, or visible grime turns luxury into humiliation — triggering Defiance spikes, Reputation Events, and public scorn.

This appendix compiles all clothing, footwear, scent, hygiene, and status-display mechanics from across the rule set into one quick-reference document. Use it during character creation, estate management, BDSM scenes, public events, and Social_Advancement checks.

Luxury Fashion Tiers
Tier 0 – Rags & Scraps
- Basic torn clothing or nakedness.
- Mechanical Effects: –2 to all social rolls, +10 daily Defiance drift for any slave wearing it, high scandal risk in public.
- Typical Items: Roughspun tunic, no footwear, burlap sack dress, bare feet caked in mud.

Tier 1 – Common Market Wear
- Simple but functional clothing.
- Mechanical Effects: No bonus, –1 to seduction/domination rolls if visibly dirty.
- Typical Items: Basic linen dress, cheap leather sandals, coarse wool breeches, plain cloth wraps, wooden clogs.

Tier 2 – Respectable Artisan
- Clean, well-fitted garments with minor adornments.
- Mechanical Effects: +1 to social rolls in merchant circles, –5 daily Defiance drift when slaves are properly dressed.
- Typical Items: Dyed wool tunic and skirt, simple leather boots, modest brass jewelry, linen shirt with basic embroidery, leather belt with small tool loops.

Tier 3 – Noble Courtesan / Merchant Luxury
- High-quality fabrics, tailored fits, subtle erotic emphasis.
- Mechanical Effects: +2 to domination and seduction rolls, +1 to Faction Reputation bleed with Silken Collar, –10 daily Defiance drift.
- Typical Items: Silk gowns with strategic cuts and side slits, heeled boots that force graceful movement, silver collars with house crests, sheer undergarments, velvet chokers with small bells, embroidered cloaks with hidden restraint loops.

Tier 4 – Elite Magnate Display
- Enchanted or masterwork items, visible symbols of ownership.
- Mechanical Effects: +3 to all social and BDSM rolls involving display, +2 to Faction Reputation with allied houses, near-zero daily Defiance drift when properly maintained.
- Typical Items: Enchanted silk restraints that double as jewelry, footwear that grants minor bonuses to posture/endurance, gem-encrusted collars, living-vine accents (Dryad-crafted), translucent spider-silk gowns, ankle cuffs with delicate chains that chime softly.

Tier 5 – Paragon Status Symbols
- Legendary or unique pieces that become known across baronies.
- Mechanical Effects: +4 to social rolls, automatic invitations to elite events, permanent +5 Reputation bleed with compatible factions, can reduce Defiance floor by 10 for displayed slaves.
- Typical Items: One-of-a-kind Pleasure Throne accessories, living-vine gowns (Dryad-crafted), soul-bound collars that echo sensation to the owner, crowns of woven night-bloom flowers that release aphrodisiac pollen, full-body harnesses of enchanted black leather with glowing runes, footwear that leaves temporary ownership marks on the ground.

Core Principles
Magic is extremely difficult to learn and wield — only 1 in 10,000 people ever master even basic rituals. Spells are never quick battlefield tools; they require specific reagents, lengthy rituals, and often dedicated spaces. Enchanted items are the only form of magic commonly encountered by the masses (usually owned by nobles, guildmasters, or wealthy slavers).
Magic exists but is rare, expensive, and dangerous. It ties directly into slavery (necromantic/conjured labor), erotic power (enchanted restraints, sensation alteration), crafting (enchanted items), and politics (mages as influential but feared figures). THE FORBIDDEN SCHOOLS
Beyond the five acknowledged schools (Enchanting, Necromancy, Illusion, Conjuration, Alteration) lie The Forbidden Schools—techniques developed in the cataclysm of the Sundering or rediscovered from pre-war heresy. These are not "utility" spells in the traditional sense; they are weapons of desperation, tools for those who would pay any price for power.

1. BLOOD MAGIC (Vitamancy)
Philosophy: The body is a battery; blood is the coin of power. Why trade gold for reagents when you can spend your own vitality?

Relation to Existing Schools: Divergent branch of Alteration and Necromancy, practiced by Crimson Veil sadists and battlefield surgeons who learned that pain and life force can substitute for costly components.

Core Mechanic: The Blood Price
Blood Magic bypasses material reagents by substituting Hit Points and Ability Score Damage. Every spell has a Blood Cost paid by the caster (or a designated sacrifice).

Blood Cost Conversion Table:

500 gp worth of reagents = 10 HP or 1 Constitution damage
1,000 gp worth of reagents = 20 HP or 2 Constitution damage
5,000+ gp worth of reagents = 50 HP or permanent -1 Constitution (cannot be healed by resting)
The Sacrifice Option: Instead of paying the cost yourself, you may draw blood from a willing or restrained subject:

Willing Subject: No additional DC; they take the damage/cost
Restrained Subject (Slave/Captive): Requires successful Grapple (Combat_Rules.txt) and Intimidation (DC 20) or Medical Restraint (Surgery DC 16 to insert siphon needles).
Defiance Impact: Sacrificing a conscious subject adds +20 Defiance to that subject immediately (terror) and +5 to all other witnesses (Medical_Trauma.txt: Witnessing Atrocities).
Blood Magic Rituals

Backlash Risk: On failure, causes Hemorrhagic Cascade—subject loses double the HP intended as blood erupts from pores (gruesome death possible)

2. TEMPORAL MAGIC (Chronomancy)

Moderate Acceleration (1 TU): Age 1 month (noticeable, -1 to Attractiveness)

Catastrophic (5+ TU): Age 5 years instantly (major trait loss)
