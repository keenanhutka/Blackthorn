FACTIONS AND POLITICS REFERENCE - Version 1.13 (70 AE)
Major factions operating in and around the Border Barony of Blackthorn Vale and the wider Shattered Principalities. Each faction has deep ties to slavery, erotic power dynamics, property management, crafting, trade, black market operations, and the post-Sundering social order. Player reputation with these factions will heavily influence social advancement, quest availability, prices, legal consequences, crafting commissions, black market access, and world events.
Reputation Scale (track in Character_Sheet or Reputation_Trackers):
Hostile (-50 to -1) → Unfriendly (0–20) → Neutral (21–50) → Friendly (51–80) → Ally (81+)
Actions that raise/lower reputation: slave/worker treatment, BDSM/training scenes, trade deals, raids, magical rituals, public ownership displays, crafting commissions, black market dealings, and faction-specific quests.
Faction Reputation Mechanics
Reputation is not just a number — it directly shapes gameplay. Each tier grants specific mechanical benefits or penalties that interact with Crafting_System, Economy_Trade_System, Slavery & Defiance, Sex/BDSM, Property_Ownership, and Crime/Justice.
Reputation Tiers & Mechanical Effects
Hostile (-50 to -1)
Severe penalties. Faction members actively work against you.
Prices for their goods/services increase 30–60%.
Quest access denied; they may offer bounties or sabotage your operations.
Black market factions may refuse deals or actively betray you.
Crafting commissions from allied noble houses are canceled or offered at ruinous terms.
Example: Hostile with Hammer & Anvil → all legitimate crafting DCs +5 and material costs +40%; hostile with Crimson Veil → erotic gear sales drop 50% and public scandals become likely.
Unfriendly (0–20)
Distrust and reluctance. Minor obstacles appear.
Prices increase 10–25%.
Limited quest access; higher DCs for social or crafting interactions.
Black market deals carry +3 to Detection rolls.
Noble commissions become rarer and demand higher proof of loyalty.
Example: Unfriendly with Silken Collar → blessed restraints cost more and may come with moral strings; unfriendly with Shadow Forge → counterfeit goods have higher flaw chance.
Neutral (21–50)
Standard business relationship. No special bonuses or penalties.
Normal prices and access.
Basic quests and commissions available.
Black market detection risk at baseline.
This is the default starting tier for most factions.
Friendly (51–80)
Positive relationship. Noticeable advantages emerge.
Prices decrease 10–25%.
Easier access to exclusive quests, better crafting commissions, and black market discounts.
Detection rolls for black market actions with allied factions gain -3 to -5.
Noble houses may offer preferential treatment or introduce you to useful contacts.
Example: Friendly with Velvet Hammer → luxury erotic crafting projects grant +2 to rolls and faster completion; friendly with Verdant Consortium → bulk slave purchases at reduced rates.
Ally (81+)
Deep alliance. Significant power and protection.
Prices decrease 25–40%; priority access to rare goods, reagents, or slaves.
Exclusive high-level quests, custom commissions, and protection from rival factions.
Black market actions with allied factions almost never trigger Detection unless catastrophic.
Noble houses may grant land, titles, or marriage alliances; guilds may fast-track your advancement.
Example: Ally with Iron Collar Guild → legal slave auctions favor you and Defiance training services are heavily discounted; ally with Whispered Ledger → near-perfect information on rival plots.
Reputation Change Guidelines
Reputation changes based on concrete actions. Changes are usually ±5 to ±20 per significant event, scaled by context and faction values.
Positive Actions (examples)
Low-Defiance slave management with good Hygiene/Scent: +10 to +20 with Broken Chain, Verdant Cycle, Evergreen.
High-quality crafting commissions delivered on time: +15 with Hammer & Anvil, Ironroot, Stormforge.
Successful BDSM training or public dominance display framed as “holy”: +10 to +25 with Silken Collar, Velvet Rose, Crimson Veil.
Profitable black market deal that doesn’t harm the faction: +10 to +30 with Shadow Forge Cartel or Whispered Ledger.
Providing rare reagents or enchanted items: +15 with Arcane Conclave or Ravenscar.
Negative Actions (examples)
Harsh cruelty or public humiliation without control: -15 to -40 with Broken Chain, Freeborn League, Verdant Cycle.
Poor Hygiene/Scent or genital size mismatch disasters in crafted goods: -10 to -25 with Silkveil, Velvet Hammer, Velvet Rose.
Failed or exposed black market run: -20 to -50 with the involved faction and possible bleed to allies.
Betraying a commission or leaking designs: -30 to -60 with the affected noble house or guild.
Using necromantic or extreme forbidden gear publicly: -20 to -40 with Verdant Cycle and Broken Chain.
Cross-Faction Reputation Bleed
Actions often affect multiple factions:
Supplying extreme BDSM gear to Bloodvein: + with Bloodvein and Silken Collar, but - with Broken Chain and Evergreen.
Flooding the market with Shadow Forge counterfeits: + with Shadow Forge Cartel, but - with Hammer & Anvil and Ironroot.
Exposing a church scandal: + with opposing church, but - with the exposed one and its noble patrons.
Reputation Recovery
Targeted quests or lavish gifts can restore 10–30 points.
Public acts aligning with faction values (e.g., funding a Silken Collar festival or aiding Broken Chain escapees) provide strong recovery.
Long-term strategy: Maintaining balanced reputation with 2–3 compatible factions while managing enemies is key to long-term success.
Iron Collar Guild
Dominant professional slavers, auctioneers, and trainers. Control legal slave markets and “re-education” services.
Goals: Maximize profit; expand debt bondage and multi-generational slavery.
Ties: Auctions, Defiance Meter, BDSM training, Economy/Trade.
Influence: Everywhere, strongest in Thornhaven.
The Verdant Consortium
Powerful alliance of large landowners, plantation barons, and estate owners who breed, raise, and trade slaves internally.
Goals: Dominate bulk slave supply and undercut the Iron Collar Guild on volume and “home-grown” quality.
Ties: Property_Ownership, large-scale vehicle caravans, generational slave lines, Hygiene/Scent management on plantations.
Influence: Strong in rural and backwards areas.
Velvet Rose Society
Secretive network of dominants, courtesans, and pleasure-house operators.
Goals: Wield soft power through seduction, blackmail, and erotic alliances.
Ties: Sex/BDSM mechanics, Relationship/Defiance, social advancement.
Influence: Strong in cosmopolitan noble circles.

Goals: End institutionalized slavery; protect escapees.
Ties: Defiance spikes, raids on property/vehicles, Crime/Justice events.
Influence: Strong in independent racial enclaves.

Crimson Watch
Professional mercenary company (mixed-race, orc-heavy).

9–11. The Three Competing Churches
Church of the Silken Collar (Debauched Pro-Slavery)
Opulent, hedonistic faith that celebrates slavery and erotic domination as sacred gifts from the gods.
Goals: Promote lavish ownership, public displays of submission, and ritualized BDSM as holy acts of devotion and pleasure.
Ties: High-end pleasure slavery, orgiastic ceremonies, enchanted restraints blessed for enhanced sensation, and moral justification for multi-generational bondage.
Influence: Very strong among decadent nobles, Velvet Rose members, and in cosmopolitan pleasure districts. Viewed as corrupt by more austere factions.
Order of the Broken Chain (Pure Anti-Slavery)
Ascetic, reformist faith that preaches liberation, equality before the divine, and the moral evil of owning another soul.
Goals: Free slaves, abolish debt bondage, and redeem society through penance and moral reform.
Ties: Underground aid networks for escaped slaves, public sermons against ownership, and quests that challenge high-defiance management or famine-era contracts.
Influence: Growing among the poor, Freeborn League sympathizers, and in some racial settlements. Actively persecuted in backwards enclaves.
Circle of the Verdant Cycle (Neutral Nature-Focused)
Mystical, nature-oriented faith that emphasizes natural cycles, seasons, growth, and balance over questions of ownership.
Goals: Preserve sacred groves, honor the turning of seasons, and maintain harmony between flesh and land.
Ties: Seasonal festivals blending sensuality and nature rites, dryad integration, Scent/Hygiene rituals tied to natural purification, and neutral stance on slavery (some members accept “natural” servitude, others quietly aid escapees).
Influence: Strong in forest enclaves and among dryads/elves; moderate and tolerated in cosmopolitan areas.
12–14. Rival Crafting Guild Factions
Iron Vein Collective (Radical Traditionalists)
Philosophy: Purity of metal and honest labor above all. They despise “frivolous” erotic or magical crafting, viewing it as decadent corruption.
Goals: Undermine “decadent” workshops through quality complaints and church lobbying (especially Broken Chain).
Ties: Exceptional durability in weapons, armor, and heavy restraints. Strong support from House Bloodvein and traditional dwarven holds.
Influence: Strong among traditional dwarven holds and militaristic houses.
Velvet Hammer (Decadent Innovators)
Philosophy: Beauty and pleasure are the highest forms of craft. They specialize in luxurious, sensual items and openly court the Velvet Rose Society and Church of the Silken Collar.
Goals: Dominate luxury and sensual crafting markets.
Ties: Masterful at high-end pleasure furniture, custom lingerie, and sensation-enhancing restraints. Excellent at noble patronage.
Influence: Strong in cosmopolitan noble circles.
Shadow Forge (Independent Underground Network)
Philosophy: Freedom from guild control. Mostly goblin, gnome, and human crafters who operate black-market workshops and experiment with forbidden combinations (including illegal magic).
Goals: Undercut guild monopolies through black-market production and rapid innovation.
Ties: Counterfeit goods, quick forgeries, experimental items; frequent smuggling.
Influence: Widespread but covert; strongest in hidden workshops.
15–18. Black Market Factions
Shadow Forge Cartel
Philosophy: Freedom through anonymity and rapid innovation. “The guild hoards knowledge; we liberate it.”
Specialties: Forged designs, quick counterfeit luxury items (especially Silkveil-style lingerie and pleasure furniture), and basic illegal enchantments.
Strengths: Fast production, excellent smuggling networks, flexible multi-skill teams.
Weaknesses: Lower quality control, frequent internal betrayals, vulnerable to guild raids.
Ties: Strongest with House Whisperveil and independent crafters.
Influence: Dominant in black market production and distribution.
Crimson Veil Syndicate
Philosophy: Pleasure is the ultimate currency. “Control the flesh and you control the soul.”
Specialties: Forbidden BDSM gear, extreme sensation enhancers, non-consensual training tools, and high-end counterfeit pleasure slaves.
Strengths: Deep connections to Velvet Rose Society and underground Silken Collar circles.
Weaknesses: High visibility and moral vulnerability; Broken Chain actively hunts them.
Ties: Works closely with House Silkveil and Bloodvein.
Influence: Strong in erotic black market and vice networks.
Bone & Blood Collective
Philosophy: Death and pain are honest labor. “Why pay living wages when the dead work for free?”
Specialties: Necromantically enhanced restraints, bone-carved pleasure items, and undead labor for dangerous crafting runs.
Strengths: Extremely cheap labor, high durability items, resistance to Defiance issues.
Weaknesses: Legal gray area; Arcane Conclave and Verdant Cycle despise them; items often carry unnatural “death scent”.
Ties: Favored by House Bloodvein and some Iron Vein traditionalists.
Influence: Strong in brutal and necromantic black market sectors.
Whispered Ledger
Philosophy: Knowledge is the real commodity. “We don’t make the goods — we make the deals.”
Specialties: Stolen guild/noble designs, blackmail packages, forged commission contracts, and discreet matchmaking between buyers and black market producers.
Strengths: Near-perfect information network, ability to launder black market profits.
Weaknesses: Little direct production capability; vulnerable to counter-espionage.
Ties: Works with all other factions as middlemen; secret ties to House Whisperveil and Ravenscar.
Influence: Pervasive but subtle across the entire black market.

Verdant Cycle acts as a moderating force, strongest in forest enclaves and among dryad communities.
