COMBAT RULES - Version 1.7 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)

Core Philosophy
Combat in the Shattered Principalities is brutal, personal, and often intertwined with power, dominance, and erotic control. Battles rarely end in clean deaths — they frequently transition into capture, restraint, training, or public humiliation. Grappling, size differences, and restraint are central mechanics, reflecting the world’s heavy emphasis on slavery, BDSM, and ownership. Combat can shift seamlessly into erotic scenes, where Pleasure Points and Defiance become as important as Hit Points.

All combat uses a d20 core resolution system. Combat is dangerous, consequential, and rarely “fair.” Poorly managed fights can result in slave escapes, property damage, reputation loss, or forced erotic submission.

Basic Combat Resolution
Initiative: d20 + Dexterity modifier (or relevant trait). Higher acts first.
Attack Roll: d20 + relevant attack bonus vs. target’s Armor Class (AC).
Damage: Weapon or unarmed damage + Strength or Dexterity modifier.
Hit Points (HP): Represent physical endurance. Reaching 0 HP renders a target unconscious or dying unless stabilized.
Movement: Standard movement speed is 30 feet per round. Difficult terrain or restraints reduce this.

Key Combat Actions
Attack: Standard melee or ranged attack.
Grapple: Special attack to restrain a target. d20 + Athletics vs. target’s Athletics or Acrobatics. Success imposes the Restrained condition.
Shove: Push a target 5–10 feet or knock them Prone.
Dodge / Disengage: Avoid opportunity attacks or reduce incoming damage.
Use Object / Item: Deploy crafted restraints, alchemical oils, or enchanted collars mid-combat.

Armor Class (AC) & Defenses
Base AC: 10 + Dexterity modifier + armor bonus.
Shields, heavy armor, and crafted restraints can modify AC.
Size difference: Larger creatures (giants) gain natural AC bonuses; smaller ones may gain evasion bonuses.

Hit Points & Conditions
Standard HP pools based on class/level or NPC stat block.
Conditions: Prone, Restrained, Grappled, Stunned, Unconscious.
Restrained condition is especially important — it transitions easily into BDSM scenes and enables domination rolls.

Erotic Combat Mechanics
Combat frequently blends with or transitions into erotic domination. When at least one participant is attempting capture, restraint, or sexual control, switch to or layer in erotic rules.

Pleasure Points (PP)
Dominant side accumulates Pleasure Points instead of (or in addition to) standard damage.
PP represents building arousal, submission, and control.
When PP reaches a target’s current Defiance threshold or a set scene value, the target may become compliant, enter a submissive state, or trigger a full BDSM scene transition.

Key Erotic Combat Modifiers
Kink Matching: Strong match grants advantage on attack/domination rolls and bonus PP.
Body Traits: High Attractiveness or favorable genital compatibility grants +2 to +4; poor Scent or high grime Hygiene imposes –2 to –4.
Crafted Items: Restraints, lubricants, or pleasure weapons provide bonuses (see Crafting_System).
Size Difference: Giants gain crushing advantage but risk accidental injury (possible backlash Defiance spike).
Environment: Private chambers grant bonuses; public fights increase scandal risk and faction reputation effects.

Defiance in Combat
Defiance is not suspended during combat — violent or humiliating actions can dramatically accelerate it. All combat actions that involve restraint, pain, public exposure, or size-mismatch automatically modify the target’s Defiance.

Defiance Modifiers During Combat
Successful Grapple or Restrain: +5 to +10 Defiance (higher if the target is publicly humiliated).
Size-mismatch attack (difference of 3+ on 1–7 scale): +8 to +20 Defiance.
Public combat or witnessed fight: +10 to +25 Defiance (especially if the slave feels degraded in front of others).
Non-lethal or capture-focused attacks: +3 to +12 Defiance (still feels like loss of agency).
Critical hit with whip, flail, or impact weapon: +10 to +15 Defiance unless followed by immediate aftercare.
Use of alchemical “fire” oils or punitive items: +15 to +30 Defiance.
Successful domination check during combat: –5 to –15 Defiance (if the scene transitions successfully).

If a slave’s Defiance reaches 76+ during combat, they gain advantage on escape attempts and may immediately trigger a Minor Rebellion Chain (per Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics). Combat Defiance spikes carry over directly into post-combat ownership and require immediate BDSM motivation scenes or aftercare to mitigate.

Expanded Combat Procedures
Combat Rounds
Each round represents approximately 6 seconds. On a creature’s turn it can move up to its speed and take one action (Attack, Grapple, Shove, Dodge, Disengage, Use Object, or Help). Bonus actions and reactions follow standard rules where applicable.

Grapple & Restraint Flow
- Successful Grapple imposes the Grappled condition.
- A second successful Grapple check against a Grappled target can impose the Restrained condition.
- Once a target is Restrained, any participant may declare a transition to a BDSM scene as a free action on their turn. The target may attempt a contested escape (Athletics or Acrobatics) as a reaction.

Non-Lethal & Capture Focus
Players may declare any attack or Grapple as non-lethal from the start. Non-lethal damage does not kill at 0 HP; instead the target falls Unconscious and becomes eligible for immediate restraint or scene transition. This is the default mode when fighting to capture slaves or enemies.

Multi-Enemy Combat Rules
When fighting multiple opponents:
- Initiative is rolled individually for each combatant.
- The dominant side may split attacks or focus fire. Grapple attempts against multiple targets require separate checks.
- Group domination is possible: one dominant may attempt to restrain or transition multiple targets if they have sufficient action economy or assistance from allies.
- Defiance spikes from combat apply individually to each slave/enemy. A single high-Defiance target reaching 76+ can still trigger a Minor Rebellion Chain even if other targets remain controlled.
- In erotic transitions, one dominant can lead a multi-target scene, but each target requires its own domination check (with possible group advantage if allies assist).

Magical Vehicle Combat Rules
When combat occurs on or from magical vehicles (enchanted airships, rift skiffs, necromantic barges, etc.):
- All normal vehicle combat rules apply.
- Magical vehicles grant advantage on one chosen combat roll per round (e.g., ramming, boarding, or escape) due to arcane assistance, but require a weekly Magic_System maintenance ritual.
- Backlash during combat: On a natural 1 on any vehicle-related roll, roll on the Magic_System backlash table. Possible effects include sudden loss of levitation, uncontrolled summoning, or mutation of slaves aboard.
- Slave-drawn magical vehicles add +10–20 Defiance per round due to arcane strain, but may allow temporary size tolerance for giant haulers via Corruption or enchantment.
- Falling from a magical vehicle adds magical fallout damage (1d6 + 1d6 force or psychic) and may trigger planar instability events.

1. CALLED SHOTS SYSTEM (Precision Maiming & Marking)
Standard attacks target the whole body. Called Shots target specific locations with mechanical trade-offs: higher risk (disadvantage or penalties) for specific grimdark outcomes (permanent maiming, BDSM capture suitability, or status degradation).

Resolution Mechanics
Declaration: Before rolling, declare the target location.
Attack Roll: Disadvantage (roll 2d20, take lower) OR -5 to hit (GM choice based on target size/mobility).
Damage: Roll normal damage + Trauma Effect based on location.
Critical Success (Natural 20): Double trauma effect; permanent scarring guaranteed.
Target Locations & Effects

2. MASS COMBAT RULES (Defiance Wave Mechanics)
When battles involve 20+ combatants (estate raids, faction wars, slave uprisings), standard initiative becomes unwieldy. Use Defiance Wave Combat.

The Defiance Wave System
Combatant Categories Divide forces into Units (groups of 5–10 similar combatants):

Elite Units: PCs, named NPCs, level 5+ characters (roll individually)
Standard Units: Guards, trained slaves, mercenaries (use group averages)
Rabble: Untrained slaves, civilians (automatically fail morale checks unless led)
The Wave Turn Instead of individual initiative, combat proceeds in Waves:

Psychological Phase (Intimidation/Leadership checks)
Missile Phase (Ranged attacks, spells)
Melee Phase (Close combat, grapples)
Morale Phase (Defiance checks, rout determination)
Unit Statistics Each unit has:

** cohesion Score** (average Defiance for slaves; morale for free): Starts at 10 + (leader's Charisma modifier)
Combat Value (CV): Average attack bonus × number of members (e.g., 5 guards with +3 attack = CV 15)
Integrity: Current cohesion × 2 = Hit Points equivalent
Resolution Mechanics

Missile Phase: Attacking unit rolls CV vs. defending unit's AC (10 + average Dexterity).

Success: Defender loses 1d6 Cohesion (casualties/desertion).
Critical Success (beat by 10+): 2d6 Cohesion lost; Defiance Spike (if slave unit, immediate +10 Defiance for survivors).
Melee Phase: Contested CV rolls (higher wins).

Winner loses 1d4 Cohesion (exhaustion/casualties).
Loser loses 2d6 Cohesion and must roll Morale.
Morale Phase (The Breaking) If Cohesion drops below 50% of starting value:

Roll d20 + leader's Intimidation vs. DC 15.
Failure: Unit Breaks (flees/surrenders).
Slave Units: Use Defiance mechanics instead - if average Defiance > 60, unit may turn on owners (Rebellion Chain activation).
Elite Intervention PCs can "attach" to a unit:

Add their personal combat stats to the unit's CV for one phase.
Can perform Called Shots (see above) against enemy leaders to reduce their unit's Cohesion by an additional 2d6 (decapitation strike).
Estate Raid Example: Attacking Force: 25 Bloodvein raiders (5 units, CV 12 each) vs. Defending Estate: 30 slave guards (6 units, CV 8 each, Cohesion 8).

Round 1:

Psychological: Bloodvein leader (Intimidation 18) vs. Slave overseer (Wis 12). Bloodvein wins; slave Cohesion drops to 7.
Missile: Raiders throw javelins (CV 12 vs. AC 12, hit). One slave unit loses 6 Cohesion (drops to 2).
Melee: Raiders charge (CV 12 vs. CV 8, raiders win). Slave unit loses 2d6 Cohesion - on a 7, reduced to negative; unit Breaks and flees.
Morale: Remaining slave units see flight; roll Cohesion checks at disadvantage (-2); two more units Break.
Result: Estate defense collapses in one round due to morale failure. Individual combat only occurs if PCs stand their ground against the raider units.

3. PSYCHOLOGICAL COMBAT (The Breaking of Will)

Pre-Combat Intimidation

Victory by 5+: Enemy suffers Shaken (-2 to all rolls for 1 minute, or until they deal damage).

4. DUEL FORMALITIES (Honor, Law, and Social Standing)
Combat between individuals of status carries weight beyond life and death. These frameworks differentiate Noble Duels (legal, prestigious) from Street Brawls (criminal, efficient).

The Noble Duel (Affairs of Honor)
Legal Framework (Crime_Justice_System.txt Integration)

Authorization: Must be sanctioned by a Noble House, the Hammer & Anvil Guild, or the Silken Collar Church (depending on faction).
Seconds: Each combatant requires a second (witness/guardian); seconds can intervene to stop dishonorable conduct.
Terms: Declared beforehand (to first blood, to submission, to death, or to "breaking" - permanent maiming).
Location: Must occur in designated dueling grounds (Noble District, Temple Quarter, or specific wilderness clearings).
Mechanical Honor Rules

The Challenge:

Issuer declares grievance (public insult, property dispute, erotic slight).
Target may Apologize (lose 1 Social Standing, -5 Reputation with issuer's faction, avoid combat) or Accept.
Refusal marks target as Coward (-10 Reputation with all martial factions, +10 Defiance from slaves who lose respect).
The Combat:

Round 1: Only melee (no grappling, no called shots to face/genitals).
Round 2+: Full combat permitted, but Called Shots to disable are honorable; Called Shots to kill only permitted if terms were "to death."
Surrender: If a combatant yields (free action), must stop immediately. Killing a surrendered duelist is Murder (Capital Crime).
The Consequences:

Winner: +5 Reputation with martial factions, +2 Social Standing if opponent was higher tier.
Loser: If "to breaking," suffers permanent injury (GM choice); if "to submission," gains Subjugated trait (-5 Defiance toward winner specifically for 1 year).
Witnesses: Bystanders gain/lose Reputation based on conduct (honorable fighting = +2, dirty tricks = -5).
Forbidden Techniques (Dishonor) Using these in a Noble Duel causes immediate Forfeit and Outcast status:

Poisoned weapons (detected via DC 18 Medicine check by seconds).
Magic (unless both parties agreed to "Arcane Duel" beforehand).
Calling shots to genitals (considered barbaric mutilation).
Attacking before the signal (seconds drop handkerchief or ring bell).
The Street Brawl (Criminal Efficiency)
Legal Status: Technically Assault (Moderate to Major Crime), but ignored in Slum districts unless property is damaged or nobles are involved.

Mechanical Differences:

No Honor: Called shots permitted immediately; poison, magic, and surprise attacks are standard.
No Seconds: No interference if one party surrenders (can be killed while yielding).
Crowd Control: Bystanders may join (1 in 6 chance per round of random bystander attacking nearest combatant).
Legal Risk: If Crimson Watch arrives (roll d20 each round: 1-3 on round 1-3, 1-5 on round 4+), all combatants face Assault charges (fines 50-200 gp, or enslavement if unable to pay).
The Vendetta Code

Unlike Noble Duels, street brawls can escalate to Blood Feuds.
If a combatant is permanently maimed or killed, their faction (gang, family, guild) gains Blood Grudge (Crime_Justice_System.txt) against the attacker.
Blood Grudge persists until:
Blood Price paid (medical costs × 10 for injury, 5000+ gp for death).
Retaliation (equivalent injury inflicted on attacker or their ally).
Guild Arbitration (Hammer & Anvil or Shadow Forge mediates for 10% of damages).
Special: The Erotic Duel (Silken Collar & Velvet Rose)
The Stakes: Not death, but dominance and public submission.

Format: Combat conducted via BDSM mechanics (Sex_BDSM_System.txt) rather than lethal attacks.
Victory: Reduce opponent to 0 Willpower (psychological surrender) or force them to Safe Word (if consensual) or Break (tears, incoherence, loss of control).
Public Display: Usually held at Grand Revels or private estates. Winner claims temporary ownership of loser (1 day to 1 week).
Mechanical Benefits: Winner gains +10 Reputation with Silken Collar; loser gains +10 Sexual Corruption (humiliation).
The Risks:

Scandal: If either participant is married/contracted to another, infidelity accusations trigger.
Physical Damage: Accidents during intense scenes (failed Called Shots during grapple) can cause actual injury (Medical_Trauma.txt).

Core Philosophy
Crafting turns raw materials, labor, time, and sometimes magic into valuable goods that drive the economy and personal power. Workshops rely heavily on slave or indentured labor, and the quality of output is directly affected by worker skill, Defiance, Hygiene/Scent management, and racial traits.
