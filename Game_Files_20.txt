1. DISTRICT INFILTRATION (The Stratified City)
Thornhaven and other Principalities capitals are vertical caste systems made physical. Moving from Southbank Slums to Noble District unobserved requires more than stealth—it requires Social Engineering, Status Display, and Institutional Bribery.

The District Security Ladder
Each district has Access Requirements and Patrol Density:

Tier 0 (Slums/Sewers)

Requirement: None (entry unrestricted)
Security: Crimson Watch patrols (1 per 100 inhabitants), gang enforcers
Risk: Random violence, disease (Medical_Trauma.txt), theft
Stealth Penalty: None (crowded chaos provides cover)
Tier 1-2 (Artisan/Trade Quarters)

Requirement: Apprentice papers or guild token (Crafting_System affiliation)
Security: Guild guards (1 per 50), Watch checkpoints at main avenues
Bribery Cost: 5-20 gp to pass checkpoint without papers
Stealth Penalty: -2 to Stealth (open streets, better lighting)
Tier 3 (Merchant/Courtesan Districts)

Requirement: Respectable clothing (Tier 2+, LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS.txt), clean Hygiene (under 40), appropriate Scent (Tier 2+ perfume)
Security: Private guards, social scrutiny ("Who is that ragged man?")
Disguise Check: DC 15 Deception to appear as servant; DC 20 to appear as minor merchant
Bribery Cost: 50-200 gp (expensive because guards risk reputation)
Tier 4-5 (Noble Districts)

Requirement: Invitation or House affiliation, Tier 4+ clothing, known face (Reputation 50+ with local nobility)
Security: House guards (2-4 per entrance), magical detection (Arcana DC 18 to spot illusions/disguises), patrol hounds
Infiltration Methods:
Servant's Entrance: DC 18 Stealth (Disguise as staff; requires stolen livery)
Rooftop: Climbing DC 20 (see Urban Exploration below)
Sewer Access: Navigation DC 16 (emerges in villa basements; 40% chance of guard encounter)
Social Engineering: Seduction/Deception DC 24 (become plus-one at party)
The Passage Mechanics
The Checkpoint Encounter When crossing between districts, roll District Alertness (d20 + district Tier):

15 or less: Clear passage
16-20: Guard scrutiny (Deception/Intimidation/Bribery check required)
21+: Stopped and questioned (DC 18 Persuasion or arrested)
Status as Armor: Your Social Standing Tier acts as automatic "disguise":

If your Tier matches/exceeds district: Automatic passage (you "belong")
If one Tier below: DC 12 Deception (act like you belong)
If two+ Tiers below: Automatic suspicion (must use Stealth/Bribery)
The Fashion Checkpoint (LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS.txt Integration) Guards make visual assessment:

Tier 0 clothing in Tier 3+ district: Immediate detention (vagrancy)
Mismatched luxury (Tier 4 coat with Tier 1 boots): DC 14 Insight check; if failed, guards assume stolen goods (confiscate clothing, -2 Social Standing for "impersonation")
Bloodstains/Scent: Hygiene over 60 or "death scent" (Necromancy) triggers Disease Protocol (Medical_Trauma.txt quarantine; 1d4 days isolation or 100 gp bribe)
Bribery Networks
The Grease (ongoing costs to operate in forbidden districts):

Weekly Pass: 50 gp (guards "forget" your face for 7 days)
Monthly Papers: 300 gp (forged guild membership; lasts 30 days, DC 15 to spot forgery)
Noble Shadow: 2,000 gp/month (minor house provides cover identity; can access Tier 4, but if caught, both you and house suffer scandal)
The Whispered Ledger (Factions_and_Politics.txt): Can arrange "ghost passage" (undocumented movement) for 10× standard bribery cost, but creates Faction Debt (must perform favor within 1 month or lose cover).

2. URBAN EXPLORATION (The Vertical City)
Thornhaven's architecture accommodates three-dimensional movement—rooftops for the desperate, sewers for the hunted, and the precarious "Middle" of interconnected balconies and bridges.

Rooftop Navigation
Movement Mechanics:

Standard Roof: Movement speed half (difficult terrain)
Slate/Tile Roofs: DC 12 Acrobatics per round or slip (prone, 1d6 falling damage)
Steep Pitched Roofs: DC 15 Athletics to climb; failure means slide (Dex save DC 12 or fall off edge)
Thatched/Weak Roofs: Risk of collapse (see below)
Rooftop Chase Rules (Combat_Rules.txt v1.7 expansion): When pursuing/fleeing across roofs:

Gap Jumps: Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) DC 10-20 depending on gap width (5 ft = DC 10, 15 ft = DC 20)
Failed Jump: Fall (damage per standard fall rules; 1d6 per 10 ft)
Pursuer Advantage: If pursuer is lighter (lower Weight spectrum, Body_Ranges_Reference.txt), gap DCs are -2 for them, +2 for heavier pursuer
Rooftop Combat:

Balance: All melee attacks at disadvantage unless both parties succeed DC 12 Acrobatics check (stance)
Shove: Special rooftop shove—target saves vs. falling off edge (Str save DC = shover's Athletics roll)
Called Shots (Combat_Expansion.txt): Target "hand" to make them drop rope/weapon (they fall if not anchored)
The High Society Bypass: Noble districts connect via "sky bridges" (covered walkways between estates):

Security: Locked (Thieves' Tools DC 20) or guarded (1 guard per bridge)
Prestige Access: If wearing Tier 4+ clothing, guards assume you belong (no check)
Dropping In: Entering buildings via rooftop hatch: DC 18 Stealth (noisy) or DC 24 Sleight of Hand (silent lockpick)
Sewer Navigation (The Undercity)
The Maze: Sewers require Survival or Engineering checks to navigate (DC 15 base, +5 if no light source).

Hazards:

Toxic Fumes: Constitution save DC 12 per hour or Poisoned (-2 to all rolls); failure by 5+ causes 1d6 poison damage (Medical_Trauma.txt: chemical exposure)
Flooding: During spring melt/rain, DC 20 Athletics to resist current or swept to drowning (underwater combat rules)
Disease: Contact with sewage requires DC 14 Con save or contract The Weeping Pox (Medical_Trauma.txt) or Cholera (rapid dehydration, -4 Con until treated)
Wildlife: Giant rats, slime molds, and Sundering Amalgams (Bestiary_Shattered_Principalities.txt) flushed from upper city experiments
Strategic Use:

Smuggling: Goods moved via sewer avoid street tolls; +50% cargo capacity but 25% spoilage rate (water damage)
Escape: Pursuers rarely follow into sewers (disgust/danger); -10 to tracking checks
Freeborn League Safehouses: Wilderness_Areas.txt cells often connect to sewer networks (DC 18 to find hidden entrances)
Building Collapse & Structural Integrity
Dilapidation Mechanics: Old buildings (Southbank slums, pre-Sundering ruins) have Structural Integrity (SI) scores:

New Construction: SI 20 (stable)
Aging: SI 10 (creaky, cosmetic cracks)
Dilapidated: SI 5 (visible sagging, dangerous)
Ruinous: SI 0 (collapse imminent)
Collapse Triggers:

Explosion/Spell Backlash: DC 15 + damage dealt vs. SI; failure means partial collapse (2d6 bludgeoning damage, DC 14 Dex save for half)
Overcrowding: If occupants exceed capacity by 50%, test SI monthly (d20 vs. SI; failure reduces SI by 1)
Earthquake/Heavy Siege: Automatic SI check; failure means collapse
Festival/Riot Collapse: During Random_Events_Table "Major Incident" (riot), buildings in affected area test SI:

Collapse: Blocks movement through street; creates cover but also traps (DC 18 Athletics to dig out; trapped characters take 1d6 damage per hour until rescued)
3. PROPERTY DEVELOPMENT (Gentrification as Warfare)
Property in Thornhaven isn't just owned—it's contested. Slum clearances, Freeborn League strongholds, and noble "urban renewal" are battles fought with deeds, arson, and midnight abductions.

Gentrification Mechanics (Slum to Respectable)
The Process: Converting a Tier 0/1 property (Slum Shack) to Tier 2+ (Respectable Workshop) requires:

Phase 1: Clearance (Driving Out Current Occupants)

Legal: Buy out tenants (200 gp per family; takes 2 months; requires Administration skill DC 16 or they refuse to leave)
Coercive: Eviction via Crimson Watch (500 gp bribe; -10 Reputation with Broken Chain; 20% chance of "accidental" fire during eviction)
Hostile: Hire thugs (Shadow Forge, 300 gp/week) to harass residents; increases Defiance of residents (+20 daily) until they flee; risk of Freeborn League Resistance (becomes combat encounter)
Phase 2: Sanitation (The Scouring)

Cost: 1,000 gp + 1 month
Mechanics: Remove "blight" (disease, vermin, Freeborn graffiti); requires Labor Block with slaves (high Defiance risk; +15 daily due to disgusting conditions)
Risk: Workers contract diseases (Medical_Trauma.txt); if 3+ workers die, property gains Haunted reputation (-2 Social Standing, attracts necromantic anomalies)
Phase 3: Construction

Standard: Follow Property_Ownership.txt upgrade rules
Expedited: Pay 50% extra to complete in half time; risk of shoddy work (SI starts at 15 instead of 20)
Phase 4: Security (Keeping the "Wrong Element" Out)

Gentrified Defense: Property gains +4 to security checks vs. infiltration (better locks, guards)
Racial Covenant: Some districts require "No Orc/Goblin" covenants (illegal but enforced by private security; -5 Reputation with racial factions, +2 with noble houses)
Defending Against Urban Renewal (The Siege of Home)
When your property is targeted by noble "urban renewal" (seizure for "public improvement" that enriches the noble):

The Eminent Domain Claim: Noble uses Eminent Domain (legal under Barony law) to seize property at "fair market value" (50% of actual value).

Defense Options:

1. Legal Battle (Crime_Justice_System.txt)

Hire Barrister: 1,000 gp upfront; extends process 3 months
Counter-Claim: Prove property has historical value (DC 22 History check to find pre-Sundering deed) or is "vital to community" (requires 20+ NPC signatures)
Bribe the Judge: 5,000 gp to noble's court; 50% chance of success (roll secretly)
2. Political Battle (Factions_and_Politics.txt)

Public Outcry: Stage protest (requires 10+ followers; DC 20 Performance to incite crowd; success adds 1 month delay and -10 Reputation to noble)
Faction Pressure: Get Iron Collar or Hammer & Anvil to claim property is "industrially vital" (requires Ally status with guild)
3. Guerrilla Warfare (Combat_Expansion.txt)

Hold Out: Refuse to leave; noble sends mercenaries (Mass Combat rules; 2-3 Standard Units vs. your forces)
Sabotage: Destroy neighboring properties (arson) to make area less attractive for renewal (major Crime, -20 Reputation, but renewal halted 1d6 months)
Freeborn Alliance: Allow Freeborn League to use property as safehouse (they defend it against mercenaries; you retain ownership but gain Seditious Property status; constant risk of Crimson Watch raids)
4. The Counter-Offer

Sell at Premium: Negotiate (DC 24 Negotiation) for 150% market value plus right to operate business in new development
Trade for Favor: Exchange property for noble's backing in Social Advancement (Tier increase) or protection of another property
The Freeborn League Cell (Urban Insurgency)
When Freeborn League occupies a slum property (Wilderness_Areas.txt integration):

Detection:

Passive: DC 20 Perception (over 1 week observation) to notice unusual traffic (escaped slaves entering)
Active: Interrogation of locals (Intimidation DC 18; they fear League reprisal)
Infiltration: Join League as sympathizer (Loyalty +10 with Freeborn, -20 with Iron Collar; risk of being branded terrorist)
Clearing the Cell:

Assault: Combat encounter (5-10 Freeborn fighters, traps, possible civilian shields)
Starvation: Siege building; cut off supplies (requires 2 weeks; Freeborn try to break out or tunnel out to sewers)
Negotiation: Persuasion DC 20 to convince them to relocate (must provide alternative safehouse or risk reputation as "slaver collaborator")
Consequences:

Success: Property becomes "Cleared" (+2 Social Standing with noble houses, +10 Reputation with Crimson Watch)
Failure (Cell Escapes): Freeborn burn property on way out (total loss); gain Blood Feud with local Freeborn chapter (Rivalry System, Stage 2)
Urban Renewal as Investment
The Block Purchase: Buy multiple adjacent slum properties simultaneously:

Cost: 60% of individual prices (bulk discount for misery)
Development: Convert to Tier 3+ housing for "respectable poor" (artisans)
Profit: Rental income 200% of slum rates; tenants have lower Defiance (grateful for improved conditions) but higher expectations (repair demands)
Risk: If you cut maintenance costs, properties revert to slum conditions in 1 year (gentrification reversed; -3 Social Standing "slumlord")

Core Philosophy
This generator provides quick, flavorful names for NPCs, slaves, freeborn citizens, nobles, and bloodline heirs in the Shattered Principalities (70 AE). Names are divided by barony/region, social class, gender presentation, and racial flavor to match the grimdark tone.

Names carry subtle mechanical flavor: noble-sounding names grant +1 to initial reputation checks with high-status factions, while lowly slave names increase starting Defiance by 2–5 unless the owner invests in re-naming rituals or luxury presentation. Expanded mechanical effects are listed for each barony and settlement.
