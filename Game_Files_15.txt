SKILLS_MASTER_LIST - Version 1.12 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
Skills represent measurable expertise that characters and NPCs develop through practice, training, risk, and consequence. In the Shattered Principalities, skills are not abstract numbers — they are hard-won tools of survival, exploitation, and power. Every skill level directly affects crafting success, combat effectiveness, BDSM outcomes, social advancement, magic aptitude, trade profits, and slave productivity.
Skill Range
0–100: Soft cap. Standard progression through repeated use, formal training, guild apprenticeship, noble commissions, or BDSM “motivation” scenes. Most mortals never exceed 80 in any skill.
101–150: Hard cap. Requires exceptional conditions (legendary quests, rare magical rituals, generational bloodlines, extreme Sexual Corruption milestones, or direct patronage from powerful mages/nobles). We will expand exact requirements later.
Above 150: Theoretically impossible without world-altering events or forbidden magic (future expansion).
Resolution Mechanic (Universal)
All skill checks use: d20 + (Skill Rank ÷ 5, rounded down) + relevant Body trait modifier (if any) + situational bonuses/penalties vs. DC.
Simple task: DC 10–15
Moderate task: DC 16–20
Difficult task: DC 21–25
Masterwork / High-stakes task: DC 26–30+
Legendary / Near-impossible task: DC 31+
Critical Success (natural 20 or beat DC by 10+): Exceptional quality, bonus effect, faster completion, or unexpected advantage (e.g., +1 permanent skill XP or reduced Defiance cost).
Failure by 5+: Wasted resources, injury risk, Defiance spike for slaves, or reputation damage.
Critical Failure (natural 1): Catastrophic result, backlash, or long-term penalty (e.g., broken tool, scandal, or +20 Defiance on a slave).
Skill Improvement Rules
Natural Use: Each successful check in a high-stakes situation grants 1–3 XP toward the skill (tracked separately per skill).
Training: 1 week of dedicated practice (or 1 BDSM motivation/reward scene) grants 5–15 XP, modified by trainer skill, workshop quality, and slave Defiance/Sexual Corruption.
Guild or Noble Tutelage: Faster XP but creates obligations or rivalries.
Sexual Corruption Synergy: Slaves or characters with 60+ Corruption gain +50% XP when improving erotic or dominance-related skills (Domination, Seduction, Carving of pleasure items, etc.).
Soft Cap (100): Further gains slow dramatically (½ XP) until special conditions unlock the 101–150 range.
Property Synergies: Working at a matching Resource & Industrial property (see Property_Ownership v1.6) grants +3 to +8 on relevant skill checks and +50% faster XP gain (e.g., Lumberjacking at a Lumber Camp or Sawmill, Trapping at a Hunting Cabin, Engineering at a Mine Shaft or Sawmill).

In 70 AE, slaves are not merely labor — they are living property, status symbols, breeding assets, pleasure tools, and constant sources of risk. Defiance is the mechanical representation of a slave’s resentment, exhaustion, and will to resist. It is never abstract: high Defiance leads to sabotage, escape attempts, poor work quality, scandals, and potential rebellion, while low Defiance enables higher productivity, loyalty, and deeper erotic submission.
Every interaction with slaves — daily labor, BDSM motivation scenes, breeding, festivals, travel, crafting, or punishment — directly modifies Defiance. The system is grimdark by design: even “successful” management carries moral weight, physical toll, and the ever-present threat that exploited bodies and minds will eventually break or strike back.

ADULT TRAIT & RACIAL MATURITY REQUIREMENT
All slavery, Defiance tracking, BDSM motivation scenes, breeding, lactation, festival participation, and related mechanics in this file apply exclusively to adult characters and NPCs only.
- “Adult” is a required binary trait that must be present on any slave (or player character) profile before any Defiance mechanics, motivation scenes, breeding rules, or related interactions can be applied.
- Maturity age (when the Adult trait is gained) varies by race and is defined in Races_Reference.txt v1.5:
  - Goblins: 12–14 years (fastest maturation).
  - Orcs and Dryads: 16–18 years.
  - Humans, Gnomes, and Giants: 18–20 years.
  - Elves: 25–30 years; Dwarves: 30–40 years (slowest due to long lifespans).
- Any slave or character lacking the “Adult” trait cannot be subject to Defiance tracking, BDSM scenes, breeding mechanics, or any related rules. Any attempt to involve non-adult individuals automatically fails, triggers severe narrative and mechanical consequences (including massive Defiance spikes, church scandals, legal penalties under Crime_Justice_System, faction backlash, or campaign-ending repercussions depending on table tone), and is strictly prohibited.
- Offspring from any successful birth begin life without the Adult trait and only gain it upon reaching their race’s maturity age. This rule is a hard prerequisite across the entire system to ensure all slavery and erotic content remains strictly adult-only.
Defiance Scale (0–100 per slave or averaged for groups)
0–20: Broken / Deeply Submissive — Maximum productivity and loyalty. Slaves anticipate owner desires and may initiate erotic service. Minimal risk of escape or sabotage.
21–40: Compliant — Reliable labor and pleasure use. Minor complaints or passive resistance possible under stress.
41–60: Restless — Noticeable drop in output, increased Hygiene/Scent issues, occasional backtalk or minor sabotage. Requires regular BDSM intervention.
61–80: Defiant — Significant productivity loss, frequent escape attempts or work slowdowns, high risk of public scandals. Punishment or intense motivation scenes become necessary.
81–100: Rebellious — Active resistance, potential violence, coordinated escape plots, or self-harm. May trigger Crime_Justice_System events or Freeborn League contact.
Defiance is tracked individually for important slaves and as a group average for large labor forces. It resets or shifts permanently based on major events (festivals, breeding success/failure, property upgrades, or repeated cruelty).

Public humiliation without aftercare: +5 to +15
Failed breeding or festival performance: +6 to +20

Resolution Mechanic for Defiance Checks
When Defiance matters (escape attempt, sabotage, work quality, erotic scene resistance), roll:
d20 + (Defiance ÷ 5, rounded down) vs. owner’s relevant skill (usually highest of Seduction, Intimidation, Flagellation, or Bondage) + situational modifiers.
Critical Success (beat by 10+ or natural 20): Slave gains temporary loyalty boost or kink discovery.
Critical Failure (fail by 10+ or natural 1): Defiance spikes +10–20 and may trigger immediate event (escape, sabotage, or Crime_Justice involvement).
BDSM Motivation Scenes (Core Tool for Defiance Management)
A dedicated scene (minimum 30–60 minutes in-game) using restraints, toys, pain, pleasure, or humiliation from BDSM_Restraints_and_Pleasure_Gear.txt and Sex_BDSM_System.txt.
Base roll: d20 + highest relevant Erotic Performance skill vs. DC equal to current Defiance ÷ 2.
Success reduces Defiance by 5–15 (scaled by scene quality, genital compatibility, and aftercare).
Failure or poor performance can increase Defiance instead.
Highly corrupt or well-matched slaves gain bonus reduction and may develop positive kinks.
Repeated identical scenes without variety cause diminishing returns (+2 DC each time).
Clothing & Footwear in Motivation Scenes: Luxury garments or high heels enhance visual appeal and Seduction/Domination rolls (+2 to +4) but add discomfort penalties if worn during intense labor or prolonged scenes (+5 to +12 Defiance unless aftercare addresses foot pain and maintenance).
Breeding & Generational Defiance
Successful breeding (per Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules) can lower a mother’s long-term Defiance floor if she feels the child will have a better life. Failed or forced breeding raises it dramatically. Offspring inherit a starting Defiance modifier based on parental treatment (+10 to +30 if parent was harshly used).
Festival & Wilderness Interactions
Participation in racial Breeding Festivals, Seduction Rites, or Strength Contests (from Wilderness_Areas.txt) creates major one-time Defiance shifts:
Strong performance in an Orc or Giant festival can drop Defiance by 15–30 but risks permanent claims or hybrid offspring complications.
Poor performance or refusal spikes Defiance by 20–40 and may trigger Blood Grudge mechanics.
Clothing & Footwear in Festivals: Festival-themed gowns or appropriate heels provide performance bonuses (+2 to +4) but risk damage or loss, potentially spiking Defiance if the slave feels humiliated by ruined luxury items.
Property & Workshop Synergies
Dedicated slave quarters, hygiene facilities, or comfort upgrades (Property_Ownership.txt) provide passive daily Defiance reduction. Overcrowded or poorly maintained properties cause steady increases. Workshops with specialized tools reduce Defiance during crafting labor.
Clothing & Footwear in Property Contexts: Luxury garments and heels require dedicated storage and maintenance facilities; neglect accelerates Hygiene-related Defiance spikes, especially in industrial or starter properties.

ESTATE MANAGEMENT INTEGRATION (New Section)
All owned properties now require active daily, weekly, and seasonal management as detailed in Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life.txt v1.2. Estate routines are a primary driver of Defiance drift and rebellion risk.
Key Mechanical Ties:
- Daily Routine Framework directly feeds into the Daily Defiance Change Factors table above. Labor Block, Maintenance Block, Interaction Block, and Recovery Block each modify Defiance based on overseer quality, security level, and beast security presence.
- Overseer NPC Mechanics (loyalty tracking, betrayal events) create secondary Defiance modifiers. A disloyal or poorly treated overseer can add +3 to +12 daily Defiance across assigned slaves.
- Estate Security Patrols and Beast Security Mechanics reduce escape probe success and raid risks but require resources and can themselves cause Defiance spikes if beasts are poorly managed or guards abuse slaves.
- Seasonal Event Tables from Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life now apply mandatory modifiers to all properties: Spring planting or Autumn harvest can lower Defiance through rewarding labor, while Winter hardship or Summer drought can cause sharp increases.
- Large or industrial properties (mines, sawmills, plantations) amplify the effects of poor estate management, making overseer loyalty and security patrols critical for preventing rebellion chains.
- Clothing & Footwear Maintenance: Explicitly part of the Maintenance Block. Neglected luxury garments or high heels accelerate Hygiene degradation and add +5 to +15 daily Defiance; proper upkeep preserves attractiveness bonuses and provides minor passive reduction.
Specific Interactions:
- Starter and Resource & Industrial properties begin with higher baseline Defiance drift until proper overseers, guards, or beast security are assigned.
- Upgrades such as Reinforced Slave Quarters or Dedicated Workshops provide explicit passive Defiance reduction only when incorporated into daily estate routines.
- Neglect of estate management (missed patrols, disloyal overseers, ignored seasonal events) directly feeds Rebellion & Escape Mechanics and can accelerate Minor → Moderate → Major chains. Neglected clothing/footwear adds an extra layer of risk during labor blocks.
- Group Motivation Scenes can now be conducted during estate Interaction Blocks with bonuses or penalties based on overseer skill and security level. High-heeled or luxury clothing enhances display-focused motivation but risks discomfort penalties.
Integration with Other Systems
Property_Ownership: Upgrades directly modify daily Defiance drift. Estate management routines are now required for full benefit from any property. Clothing & Footwear Display & Maintenance Upgrades tie directly into prestige and upkeep.

Economy_Trade_System & Crafting_System: High Defiance reduces output and increases sabotage risk. Estate management quality directly affects passive income and workshop tiers. Luxury clothing and heels add market value premiums but require upkeep.
Wilderness_Areas & Random_Events_Table: Festivals and enclave encounters cause major one-time swings. Estate security and beast patrols help mitigate wilderness spillover. Festival-themed gowns and heels provide bonuses but risk damage leading to Defiance spikes.
Magic_System: Forced rituals or Alteration can spike or suppress Defiance with backlash risk, especially during estate security incidents.
Crime_Justice_System: Extreme Defiance can trigger investigations or public punishments, worsened by failed estate security.
Factions_and_Politics: Slave treatment and estate management style heavily influence reputation with churches and noble houses. Loyal, well-managed estates grant positive reputation bleed; chaotic or neglectful ones accelerate scandals. Luxury clothing and heels boost decadent factions but increase scandal risk with reformists.
Daily Slave Management Framework
Slaves require structured routines to keep Defiance manageable. A typical day includes:
Labor Block (4–10 hours): Type of work (mining, crafting, agriculture, pleasure service) directly affects Defiance drift. High heels or restrictive luxury clothing during labor add +5–15 Defiance from discomfort.
Maintenance Block (Hygiene, meals, rest): Neglect here causes automatic +3 to +8 daily increase. Clothing and footwear upkeep is now mandatory to prevent Hygiene degradation and retain attractiveness bonuses.
Interaction Block (owner oversight, training, or BDSM): Primary opportunity to reduce Defiance.
Recovery Block (sleep): Poor conditions add +2 to +5 overnight.
Owners may assign “overseers” (trusted slaves or hired guards) to manage groups, but overseer skill and treatment of under-slaves creates secondary Defiance modifiers.
Motivation Scene Frameworks (Detailed Examples)
Punitive Scene (high Flagellation/Bondage focus)
Ideal for high-Defiance slaves. Success reduces Defiance by 8–15 but risks +5 backlash if aftercare is skipped.
Best used sparingly; repeated use raises permanent floor.
Pleasure Conditioning Scene (high Seduction/Oral/Vaginal focus)
Best for mid-range Defiance. Combines pleasure with light restraint. Can also discover or deepen kinks.

Uses positive reinforcement. Lower risk, slower but more stable Defiance reduction. High Sexual Corruption slaves respond exceptionally well.
Group Motivation Scene (for workshops or caravans)
One high-skill scene can affect multiple slaves, but average skill of the group sets the base DC. Public nature adds reputation risk/reward.
Clothing & Footwear in Motivation Scenes: Luxury garments or high heels enhance visual appeal (+2 to +4 Seduction/Domination) but add discomfort penalties (+5–12 Defiance) unless aftercare addresses foot pain and maintenance.
Aftercare Rules
Every motivation or punishment scene requires an Aftercare roll (d20 + Seduction or Medicine vs. DC 12 + scene intensity). Failure adds +3 to +8 Defiance the next day and may cause lasting psychological effects (temporary skill penalties or kink aversion).
Clothing & Footwear in Aftercare: Removing high heels or cleaning luxury garments during aftercare grants +2 to +4 to the Aftercare roll and reduces next-day Defiance spikes.
Rebellion & Escape Mechanics
When a slave (or group average) reaches 81+ Defiance, roll a daily Rebellion Check:
d20 + (Defiance ÷ 5) vs. owner’s highest control skill + property security bonus.
Success: Minor incident (slowdown, theft, or complaint).
Failure by 5+: Coordinated escape attempt or sabotage.
Critical Failure: Full rebellion — may spread to other slaves, trigger Freeborn League contact, or escalate to Crime_Justice_System events.
Clothing & Footwear in Rebellion: Restrictive luxury garments or high heels can hinder escape attempts (mobility penalty) but also provide visual identifiers for recapture or increase Defiance if slaves feel humiliated by damaged status symbols.
Freeborn League Rescue Events
In wilderness or border zones, high-Defiance slaves have a chance each day to attract external aid. Success on a hidden League roll can turn a single escape into a larger raid or safehouse extraction.
Generational Defiance Inheritance
Offspring start with Defiance equal to the mother’s current floor ±10, modified by:
Harsh treatment during pregnancy: +15 to +30
Kind or rewarding treatment: –10 to –20
Festival or ritual participation during gestation: special modifiers (see Wilderness_Areas breeding festivals)
Clothing & Footwear in Generational Defiance: Well-dressed lactating mothers in comfortable quarters lower inherited Defiance floors; neglected luxury items during nursing raise them through observed discomfort.
Bloodline Traits
Exceptional bloodlines (from repeated high-Corruption or festival breeding) can have reduced baseline Defiance floors or bonus resistance to certain motivation types.
Sample Rebellion Event Chains
These escalating chains activate when Defiance thresholds are crossed or critical rebellion checks fail. They provide narrative structure and tie directly into other systems. Each chain can be interrupted by successful motivation scenes, bribes, or interventions. Chains are modular — stages can be skipped or accelerated based on player actions, festival outcomes, or wilderness events.
Minor Rebellion Chain (Defiance 61–80 or failed minor check)
Slowdown & Complaints: Work output drops 20–30%. Slaves voice grievances openly. Hygiene/Scent worsens.
Minor Sabotage: Tools break, goods are damaged, or small theft occurs. Triggers Economy_Trade_System loss or Crafting_System quality penalty.
Escape Probe: One or two slaves test security (e.g., during travel or wilderness work). Success leads to Minor Rebellion Chain escalation; failure allows owner intervention.
Clothing & Footwear Note: Damaged luxury garments or uncomfortable heels during labor can accelerate complaints and sabotage.
Resolution: Strong BDSM scene or punishment can de-escalate. Weak response pushes to Moderate Chain.
Moderate Rebellion Chain (Defiance 81–95 or failed moderate check)
Coordinated Resistance: Group work refusal or deliberate low-quality output. Affects multiple slaves or entire workshop.
Internal Agitation: Slaves begin spreading dissent; possible Freeborn League contact in border zones. Defiance rises +5 daily for the group.
Targeted Sabotage or Theft: Valuable goods, Property_Ownership upgrades, or vehicles are damaged/stolen. May trigger Crime_Justice_System investigation.
Partial Escape Attempt: 1–3 slaves flee during a window of opportunity (night travel, festival chaos, or wilderness exploration).
Clothing & Footwear Note: Restrictive heels or torn luxury dresses can hinder or aid escapes, adding narrative flavor and Defiance modifiers.
Resolution: Public punishment, high-intensity motivation scenes, or concessions (better conditions) can halt the chain. Failure escalates to Major Rebellion.
Major Rebellion Chain (Defiance 96–100 or critical failure)
Open Revolt: Slaves actively resist orders, attack overseers, or destroy property. Immediate combat or chase scene likely.
Mass Escape or Uprising: Large group attempts breakout, potentially linking with Freeborn League cells or nearby racial enclaves (especially orcs/giants in wilderness).
Scandal Cascade: Public exposure of poor treatment damages owner reputation with churches, noble houses, or guilds (Factions_and_Politics shifts –20 to –50).
Full Consequences: Possible property seizure, slave loss, legal penalties (Crime_Justice_System), or forced alliance with Freeborn League to survive backlash.
Clothing & Footwear Note: Destroyed luxury garments or broken heels become symbols of rebellion, amplifying scandal and reputation loss.
Resolution: Extreme measures (lethal force, mass punishment, or magical suppression) carry heavy moral and reputational cost. Successful de-escalation requires multiple high-DC motivation scenes, concessions, or external aid.

Magic_System Effects on Rebellion
Magic interacts powerfully with Defiance and rebellion:
Alteration rituals used for control can temporarily lower Defiance (–10 to –20) but carry high backlash risk that may cause sudden spikes, mutations, or unintended summoning events during a rebellion chain.
Necromantic bindings or Conjuration summons can suppress open revolt but often create undead or planar complications that escalate the chain into a magical catastrophe.
Enchanting restraints or Illusion-based motivation scenes provide strong short-term reduction but risk permanent Corruption side effects or illusionary “ghost rebellions” that haunt the property.
High backlash from any ritual during a Major Rebellion stage can trigger catastrophic events (mass summoning, planar rifts, or curse outbreaks) that affect the entire holding and surrounding wilderness.
Clothing & Footwear in Magic Effects: Enchanted heels or garments can add subtle effects (vibration, illusion) but amplify backlash risk, especially if damaged during a rebellion.
Rebellion Resolution Tables
Use these tables when a Rebellion Check fails or a chain reaches a critical stage. Roll once per stage or when the GM deems appropriate. Modifiers include owner’s relevant skill, property security upgrades, recent BDSM success, festival performance, and current Defiance level.
Minor Rebellion Resolution Table (d20)
1–4: Quick Compliance – Slaves back down after a single firm warning or short punitive scene. Defiance drops –8. No further escalation.
5–8: Grudging Acceptance – Minor concessions (extra rest or better food) required. Defiance drops –5. Chain stalls for 1–2 days.
9–12: Sabotage Incident – Minor damage or theft occurs. Economy_Trade_System or Crafting_System penalty. Defiance +3.
13–16: Escape Probe Succeeds Partially – 1 slave flees but is recaptured. Triggers Moderate Chain if not handled immediately.
17–20: Dissent Spreads – Whispers reach other slaves or Freeborn contacts. Defiance +5 for the group; chain escalates to Moderate.
Moderate Rebellion Resolution Table (d20)
1–3: Strong Intervention Works – High-DC group BDSM scene or public punishment halts the chain. Defiance drops –10 to –15.
4–7: Costly Concessions – Owner must grant better conditions or festival participation rights. Defiance drops –8 but Reputation may suffer minor hit.
8–11: Targeted Sabotage – Significant loss (vehicle damage, stolen goods, or workshop setback). Triggers Crime_Justice_System roll.
12–15: Partial Mass Escape – 2–5 slaves flee. Possible Freeborn League rescue event. Chain moves toward Major if more than half escape.
16–20: Uprising Sparks – Open resistance begins. Immediate combat or chase. Defiance spikes +10; chain escalates to Major.
Major Rebellion Resolution Table (d20)
1–2: Miraculous De-escalation – Exceptional roleplay, massive concessions, or external aid (e.g., allying with a rival faction) stops the revolt. Defiance drops –20 but leaves permanent scars (higher floors or grudges).
3–6: Brutal Suppression – Lethal force or magical rituals end the uprising. Heavy moral cost, Reputation loss (–20 to –40 with reformist factions), and possible permanent Defiance floors for survivors.
7–10: Costly Compromise – Owner loses significant property, slaves, or status to buy peace. Possible forced alliance with Freeborn League or local enclaves.
11–14: Full Escape & Raid – Large group escapes and returns with allies (Freeborn, orcs, giants). Property damage or loss likely. Triggers major Factions_and_Politics and Crime_Justice events.
15–18: Scandal Explosion – Public exposure leads to noble/church investigation. Possible asset seizure or exile-level consequences.
19–20: Catastrophic Collapse – Total slave loss, property destruction, or player death/capture. Campaign-altering grimdark outcome.
Festival-Specific Resolution Modifiers
Successful festival performance: –5 to all resolution rolls (owner appears strong).
Poor festival performance or refusal: +5 to +10 to resolution rolls (slaves feel emboldened).
Racial grudge mechanics active (Orc Blood Grudge, Giant Blood Grudge, etc.): Additional +5 penalty.
Clothing & Footwear in Festival Resolution: Damaged festival gowns or heels can amplify humiliation and add +5 to resolution penalties.
Faction Politics & Rebellion Interactions
Slave rebellions do not occur in a vacuum — they are heavily influenced by the complex politics of the five baronies and major factions (Factions_and_Politics.txt). High Defiance or active rebellion chains can trigger faction-specific responses, reputation shifts, and cascading consequences.
Silken Collar Church Influence
Pro-slavery stance: Views well-managed rebellion as proof of owner weakness. Successful suppression grants +10 to +30 Reputation with Silken Collar; failure causes public scandal and –20 to –40.
Erotic framing: Church may demand “holy correction” scenes or ritualized punishments, offering mechanical bonuses to motivation rolls but increasing scandal risk.
Broken Chain Church Influence
Anti-slavery reformers: High Defiance or rebellion events grant them ammunition for sermons and investigations. Each stage of rebellion grants +10 to +25 Reputation with Broken Chain if the player is seen as cruel.
Intervention: Reformist agents may secretly aid escapes or leak evidence, turning a personal rebellion into a barony-wide political crisis.
Verdant Cycle Church & Dryad Alliances
Nature-balance focus: Rebellions in Verdant March wilderness often attract dryad intervention (vines aiding escapes, fertility curses). Successful resolution through “natural” rites grants +15 to +40 Reputation; harsh suppression damages alliances.
Breeding angle: Rebellion during or after breeding festivals can lead to dryad claims on hybrid offspring.
Hammer & Anvil Guild & Noble Houses (Ironroot, Silkveil, etc.)
Economic pragmatism: Rebellion disrupts workshops, trade, or property value. Each stage causes Economy_Trade_System losses and possible guild blacklisting (–10 to –30 Reputation with Hammer & Anvil).
Political leverage: Rival noble houses may exploit visible rebellions to launch scandals or seize assets, especially if the player is seen as weak.
Freeborn League
Direct catalyst: High-Defiance slaves in wilderness or border zones have increased chance of League contact. Successful rebellion stages can escalate into League-backed raids or safehouse extractions.
Reputation bleed: Helping suppress a League-aided rebellion grants +20 to +50 with pro-slavery factions but –30 to –60 with reformists and independents.
Cross-Faction Bleed & Long-Term Effects
Reputation from rebellion handling bleeds across allied factions (e.g., suppressing a rebellion harshly boosts Silken Collar but damages Verdant Cycle).
Repeated rebellions can create permanent “notorious owner” status, increasing future Defiance drift for all new slaves and locking out certain commissions or alliances.
Positive handling (fair resolution, festival integration, or corruption paths) can create loyalist bloodlines or faction patronage.
Clothing & Footwear in Long-Term Effects: Chronic neglect of luxury garments or heels on high-value slaves can create a visible “notorious” marker, amplifying reputation damage and future Defiance drift.
Integration Expansions
Vehicle_System: Cramped travel conditions add +5 to +15 daily Defiance for slave-drawn or passenger slaves. Restrictive heels or luxury clothing exacerbate this.
Crafting_System: High-Defiance labor forces increase project DCs and lower quality tiers. Clothing/footwear maintenance affects workshop output through Hygiene.
Economy_Trade_System: Slaves with 60+ Defiance reduce market value and increase upkeep costs. Luxury clothing and heels add value premiums but require upkeep.
