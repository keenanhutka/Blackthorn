CRIME & JUSTICE SYSTEM - Version 1.8 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
Justice in the Shattered Principalities is not blind — it is bought, influenced, and weaponized by the powerful. Seventy years after the Sundering and the 46 AE famine, laws exist primarily to protect property, slavery contracts, noble privilege, and guild monopolies. Enforcement is inconsistent, corrupt, and often eroticized: public punishments frequently involve humiliation, restraint, or forced displays of submission.
Crimes against slaves are rarely prosecuted unless they damage valuable property or cause public scandal. Crimes by slaves are punished harshly to deter defiance. Black market activities, illegal magic, and forbidden crafting sit in a gray zone where punishment depends more on who you know than what you did. The system is deliberately designed to keep the poor and powerless in check while allowing nobles and wealthy mages to operate with near-impunity.
Crime Categories & Examples
Crimes are divided into tiers with typical punishments that scale with social status, faction reputation, and economic impact.
Minor Crimes (Fines or short confinement)
Petty theft, minor tax evasion, public drunkenness, small-scale smuggling of common goods.
Example: Selling low-quality counterfeit restraints without guild approval.
Moderate Crimes (Flogging, short enslavement, or heavy fines)
Assault, forgery of minor documents, unlicensed crafting, poor slave treatment that causes visible damage, illegal use of basic alchemical items.
Example: Using flawed black market sex items that cause injury during a noble gathering.
Major Crimes (Long-term enslavement, public branding, or execution)
Slave rebellion aid, large-scale black market operations, unauthorized necromancy or conjuration, kidnapping free citizens for slavery, deliberate sabotage of guild workshops.
Example: Running a Shadow Forge operation that floods the market with forged Silkveil lingerie.
Capital / Heretical Crimes (Public execution, soul-binding, or permanent enslavement)
Mass defiance incitement, forbidden magical alteration of nobles, church desecration, large-scale demonic summoning, or exposing high-level noble scandals through black market means.
Example: Creating and distributing mutagenic beauty serums that permanently disfigure a noble’s pleasure slaves.
Enforcement & Punishment Mechanics
Enforcement Bodies: Crimson Watch (day-to-day), guild enforcers (crafting violations), church inquisitors (moral crimes), and noble house guards (personal matters).
Trial Process: Quick and biased. Nobles and high-reputation individuals often avoid trial through bribes or influence. Slaves and low-status individuals receive summary judgment.
Punishment Modifiers:
High faction reputation can reduce severity or convert punishment to fines/“community service” (often erotic labor).
Public scandals or church involvement increase severity.
Evidence of good Hygiene/Scent management or low Defiance can mitigate slave-related punishments.
Common Punishments
Fines: Scaled to crime severity and offender wealth (10–5,000+ gp).
Flogging or Public Restraint: Often combined with erotic humiliation; increases Defiance for slaves.
Temporary Enslavement: Debt bondage or “re-education” contracts (common for moderate crimes).
Branding or Collar of Shame: Permanent mark that lowers social standing and increases future Detection risk.
Execution or Soul-Binding: Reserved for capital crimes; may involve public BDSM-style spectacle for Silken Collar audiences.

Barony-Specific Justice Styles (70 AE)
Justice varies dramatically across the five baronies, reflecting local power structures, church influence, and economic priorities.
Thornhaven (Blackthorn Vale): Cosmopolitan but corrupt. Trials are public spectacles; Silken Collar often frames erotic punishments as “holy penance.” Noble houses can buy leniency easily. Focus on reputation and scandal avoidance.
Jarick (Ironvein Barony): Brutal and industrial. Guild enforcers (Hammer & Anvil) handle most cases. Punishments emphasize labor restitution (forced mine work). Necromantic labor is sometimes used as “community service.” Very little mercy for sabotage.
Moonstone (Silken Reach Barony): Scandal-driven and theatrical. Silken Collar church heavily influences trials. Punishments are often public erotic displays or temporary pleasure-slave contracts. High-status offenders are rarely executed — they are humiliated instead.
Lariss (Verdant March Barony): Nature-focused and ritualistic. Verdant Cycle priests act as judges. Punishments frequently involve “restorative” labor in groves or forced breeding as penance. Environmental crimes (damaging sacred groves) carry the harshest sentences.
Gurt (Bloodthorn Barony): Lawless and violent. House Bloodvein and raider lords dispense summary justice. Public floggings, arena combats, and summary enslavement are common. Freeborn League activity makes many trials into political statements.
Detailed Trial & Evidence Mechanics
Summary Judgment (Most Common)
Used for slaves, poor freemen, and minor/moderate crimes.
Duration: Immediate (minutes to hours).
Process: Local enforcer or noble guard pronounces guilt on the spot. Evidence is optional; testimony from free citizens or guild members is automatically accepted.
Player Interaction: High-Reputation or high-Seduction characters may attempt to negotiate on the spot (DC 18–25).
Formal Trial (Rare, for Moderate+ Crimes Involving Free Citizens or High-Value Property)
Duration: 1–3 days.
Location: Usually in a public square, guild hall, or church court (varies by barony).
Process:
Accuser presents evidence (often bribed witnesses or magical recordings).
Accused may speak or present counter-evidence (rarely successful without bribes or allies).
Judge (noble, guild master, or church inquisitor) rules.
Sentence is carried out immediately or within 24 hours for maximum public impact.
Mechanical Modifiers: +5 to +15 to all social rolls if the accused has Ally reputation with the judge’s faction; –5 to –15 if Hostile.
Evidence & Manipulation Rules
Slave testimony requires magical compulsion or torture (high risk of false confessions).
Magical evidence (Illusion recordings, Alteration traces) is powerful but can be countered with counter-rituals (Magic_System backlash risk).
Black market items or enchanted goods automatically trigger guild or church involvement.
High Sexual Corruption can be cited as “mitigating evidence of natural depravity” in erotic crimes.
Slavery Contract Details
Slavery contracts are the legal backbone of the economy and the most common form of punishment or debt resolution.
Types of Contracts
Debt Bondage (Famine-Era Contracts): Inherited from the 46 AE famine; multi-generational and nearly impossible to break without manumission or church intervention.
Temporary Enslavement Contracts: Used as punishment or voluntary debt repayment (1–12 months typical).
Breeding/Lineage Contracts: Common in Lariss and among the Verdant Consortium; offspring are automatically enslaved.
Pleasure-Service Contracts: Common in Moonstone and Thornhaven; often include BDSM training clauses.
Mechanical & Legal Features
Owner gains full Property_Ownership rights over the contracted individual.
Contracted slaves start with 30–60 Defiance; daily growth +5–15 unless mitigated by BDSM scenes.
Breaking a contract (escape, sabotage) is a Major or Capital crime for the slave and may trigger pursuit by Crimson Watch or guild enforcers.
Manumission: Extremely expensive (5,000–20,000+ gp) and requires high faction reputation or church approval.
Transfer/Sale: Contracts can be bought, sold, or gifted like property; high-value slaves with good Hygiene/Scent or skills command premium prices.
Erotic & Grimdark Elements
Contracts often include explicit clauses for “training,” “discipline,” or “pleasure service.”
Public signing or transfer ceremonies are common spectacles that raise or lower faction reputation.
High Sexual Corruption slaves may negotiate better terms or even turn contracts into opportunities for influence.
Barony Variations
Thornhaven: Contracts are formal and often include pleasure clauses.
Jarick: Contracts emphasize labor quotas with harsh penalties for failure.
Moonstone: Pleasure-service contracts are luxurious but scandal-prone.
Lariss: Breeding contracts are sacred under Verdant Cycle doctrine.
Gurt: Contracts are informal and often enforced by brute force.

1. SUPPLY CHAIN INTERRUPTIONS (The Fragile Web)
The Shattered Principalities' economy depends on vulnerable supply lines: ore from Jarick mines, silk from Moonstone plantations, reagents from Verdant March groves. When these break, prices spiral and desperate measures become necessary.

The Supply Chain Event System
Trigger: Roll d20 at the start of each month (or when GM determines), adding modifiers:

Winter: +2 (harsh conditions)
War/High Tension: +3 (faction conflicts)
Over-exploitation: +5 (if Resource & Industrial properties were pushed beyond capacity last month)
Supply Chain Event Table (d20)

1-4: Natural Disaster

Mine Collapse (Ironvein): Blacksmithing materials (iron, coal) prices ×2 for 1d4 months. All Modest Mine Shaft properties suffer -50% output and +15 daily Defiance (unsafe conditions). DC 22 Engineering to clear; failure causes another collapse (lose 1d10 slaves).
Crop Blight (Verdant March): Herbalism/Alchemy reagents (fertility tonics, scent herbs) prices ×3. Established Lumber Camp properties near affected areas gain +20% income (clearing dead wood) but risk Planar Contagion (Medical_Trauma.txt) if blight was magical.
River Flood: Watermill properties destroyed (1d6×1000 gp damage). Trade routes via river blocked 1d6 weeks; all goods transported by river cost +50%.
Deep Freeze: Northern trade halts. Winter clothing/salted meat prices ×2. Slaves without warm clothing (Tier 3+ heavy garments) suffer +10 daily Defiance.
5-8: Faction Sabotage

Guild Embargo: Hammer & Anvil cuts off rival houses. Metal goods prices ×1.5; Shadow Forge black-market prices ×2 (profiteering).
Freeborn League Raid: Major plantation burned. Sugar/spice supply drops 40%; prices ×2. Slave market panics (+20% prices due to "scarcity mentality" even if supply exists).
Church Interdiction: Silken Collar declares luxury goods "sinful" for 1 month. Silk, perfumes, pleasure items prices crash (-50%); merchants hoard, causing artificial shortage next month (prices ×3).
Noble House Blockade: House Thorne seizes trade roads. All Vehicle_System travel through their territory requires 10% tariff or faces "bandit attacks" (actually Thorne soldiers).
9-12: Infrastructure Failure

Bridge Collapse: Specific trade road segment destroyed. No travel until repaired (DC 24 Engineering, 2 weeks, 500 gp materials). Toll income lost; diverted caravans add +5 days to all journeys.
Forge Fire: Major workshop burns. Custom crafting orders delayed 1 month; existing contracts breached (reputation loss per Factions_and_Politics.txt).
Warehouse Rot: Poorly maintained storage ( neglected Property_Ownership Maintenance Block) destroys 1d10% of stored goods. High-value alchemical reagents may become unstable (explosion risk: DC 15 Alchemy to stabilize or lose 2d6 HP in blast).
Slave Exodus: Mass escape from major estate (Rebellion Chain Major success). Labor shortage causes +30% wage costs for free workers; Defiance floors rise +5 region-wide (fear spreads).
13-16: Magical Contamination

Rift Residue: Transported planar goods (from Conjuration caravans) leak. Random Magic_Backlash_Table effects on cargo handlers. Arcane Conclave quarantines goods for 1d4 weeks.
Necrotic Taint: Corpse Preservation Elixir batch fails. "Death scent" contaminates luxury textiles (LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS.txt); -4 Attractiveness penalty for wearing affected garments.
Alchemical Reaction: Unstable reagents mix in transit. Explosion destroys 1d6 cargo slots; fire spreads to properties (DC 18 to contain).
Wild Magic Storm: Airship/Vehicle_System magical transport disrupted. All enchanted cargo arrives 1d6 days late or mutated (roll on Body_Ranges_Reference for random trait shifts).
17-20: No Event (Stable)

Or GM Choice: Minor delay (1-2 days) for flavor.
The Scarcity Cascade Effect
When a specific good becomes scarce (price ×2 or higher), it triggers Secondary Scarcities:

Iron Shortage →

Construction halts (-20% Property upgrade speed)
Shadow Forge counterfeit iron floods market (50% chance of flawed goods per Crime_Justice_System.txt)
Herb Shortage (Scent Weaver ingredients) →

Perfume/Tier 4+ clothing maintenance impossible without supply
High-society slaves lose Attractiveness bonuses (-2)
Silken Collar church attendance drops (can't maintain "divine scent"); Broken Chain gains converts (+1 Reputation)
Slave Shortage (Labor crisis) →

Wages for free workers ×2 (increased demand)

2. BANKING & DEBT MECHANICS (The Iron Collar of Finance)
Seventy years after the Famine, debt remains the primary shackle. The Iron Collar Guild operates the largest "lending houses," but nobles and Shadow Forge cartels offer alternatives with... different terms.

Loan Types & Usury Rates
Legitimate Banking (Iron Collar Guild, House Stormforge)

Standard Estate Mortgage:

Amount: Up to 80% of Property_Ownership value (e.g., 8,000 gp loan on 10,000 gp estate)
Interest: 5% monthly compound (60% annual)
Collateral: The estate itself. Miss 3 payments = foreclosure and immediate Debt Bondage (see below).
Terms: 12-24 months. Early repayment allowed (no penalty).
Trade Credit (Merchant Houses):

Amount: 1,000–10,000 gp for specific cargo runs
Interest: 10% flat (due on completion of trade run)
Risk: If cargo lost (bandits, shipwreck), debt remains. Debt Insurance available (15% of cargo value) from Whispered Ledger (50% chance they pay out; 50% chance they claim "act of god").
Emergency Loans (Noble Houses):

Amount: Varies (political leverage determines limit)
Interest: 0% monetary, but Favor Debt (Factions_and_Politics.txt: owe specific quest/complicity)
Collateral: Blood oath; failure to repay allows creditor to claim one bloodline member as slave (legal under Famine Contracts).
Shadow Banking (Crimson Veil, Shadow Forge)

Black Market Credit:

Amount: Unsecured up to 5,000 gp (no questions asked)
Interest: 20% weekly compound (exponential growth)
Enforcement: Physical violence (Medical_Trauma.txt injuries), destruction of property, or Organ Harvesting (permanent -2 to Constitution if they take "payment" in flesh).
Soul Bonds (Arcane Conclave):

Amount: 10,000+ gp
Interest: 1% monthly, but paid in soul essence (harvested via Necromancy rituals). Failure to pay turns debtor into undead thrall under creditor's control.
Compound Interest Mechanics
Track loans on Monthly Accounting (Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life.txt):

Example: 5,000 gp loan at 5% monthly compound:

Month 1: 5,000 + 250 = 5,250 due
Month 2: 5,250 + 262 = 5,512 due
Month 6: 6,700 due (34% increase)
Month 12: 8,950 due (79% increase)
Minimum Payments: Most lenders require interest-only payments monthly. Principal remains forever unless specifically paid down.

Default Consequences:

Stage 1 (1 missed payment):

Civil Penalty: -2 Social Standing ("unreliable debtor")
Property Lien: Cannot sell estate until debt cleared
Defiance Impact: Slaves learn of financial trouble; +5 daily Defiance (fear of being sold to cover debts)
Stage 2 (3 missed payments):

Foreclosure: Property seized by creditor (lose all estate benefits)
Asset Liquidation: All slaves auctioned (Economy_Trade_System.txt prices -20% for quick sale)
Personal Debt: Remaining balance converts to Indentured Servitude (see below)
Stage 3 (6+ missed payments):

Criminal Status: Declared "Famine Debtor" (reference 46 AE contracts)
Debt Slavery: Character becomes legal slave of creditor (Defiance tracked normally, but no income; all labor value goes to debt).
Generational Transfer: If PC dies while enslaved, debt passes to offspring (Legacy_Tracker.txt: inherited Defiance floor +20, starting with "Debt Bondage" trait).
The Debt Slavery Contract
When a free character cannot pay debts, they may sign (or be forced to sign) a Debt Slavery Contract (Crime_Justice_System.txt: Temporary Enslishment variant).

Terms:

Duration: Until debt + interest paid (often impossible due to compound rates).
Labor Value: 5 sp per day (standard slave labor). Against a 10,000 gp debt, this requires 200 years to pay off.
Sexual Corruption: Starts at 0 but rises rapidly (+2 per week) due to humiliation; high Corruption may earn "privileges" (better food, clothing) but locks in psychological dependency.
Escape: Legal escape requires full repayment (impossible) or Manumission (5,000–20,000 gp to buy freedom, per Factions_and_Politics.txt).
Resistance: Defiance grows normally; if reaches 76+, triggers Debtor's Rebellion (specific Rebellion Chain variant where creditors are targeted for assassination).
PC Debt Slavery Play: If a player character becomes a debt slave:

Continue playing, but all income goes to creditor
Must perform daily estate labor (use Labor Block mechanics)
Can secretly earn "hidden income" (DC 20 Stealth/Deception weekly) to accumulate escape funds
High Sexual Corruption path may lead to becoming "trusted house slave" with eventual opportunity to betray/escape
3. MARKET MANIPULATION (Economic Warfare)
Sophisticated players can weaponize the market, creating artificial shortages or gluts to destroy rivals.

The Pump-and-Dump Scheme
Step 1: Accumulation (Months 1-2)

Secretly buy up all available stock of a specific good (e.g., Grave Warden's Ash for necromantic rituals).
Detection DC: 15 (Economy skill) for rivals to notice unusual buying patterns.
Cost: Requires 10,000+ gp liquid capital to corner a minor market, 50,000+ for major goods (iron, silk).
Step 2: The False Scarcity (Month 3)

Spread rumors of shortage (Deception DC 18, or use Whispered Ledger contacts).
Effect: Prices rise 50% (Minor Surplus) to 200% (Major Shortage) on Economy_Trade_System tables.
Rival Response: Other merchants may release reserves to capitalize; roll d20: 1-5 they flood market and collapse your scheme (prices drop to 50%).
Step 3: The Dump (Month 4)

Sell accumulated stock at inflated prices.
Profit: (New Price - Original Price) × Quantity.
Example: Bought 100 units at 100 gp, sold at 300 gp = 20,000 gp profit (minus 10,000 cost = 10,000 gp net).
Step 4: The Aftermath

Market crashes (prices drop to 25% of normal for 1d6 months as supply exceeds demand).
Rival Ruin: Any merchant holding that good loses massive value; may go bankrupt (trigger Debt Slavery above).
Reputation: +10 with Shadow Forge (ruthless), -10 with Hammer & Anvil (disrupted legitimate trade), -20 with affected noble houses (if you ruined their finances).
Short Selling (The Shadow Bet)
Bet against future prices:

Borrow goods now (promise to deliver in 3 months), sell immediately at current price.
Hope prices drop; buy back cheaper in 3 months to fulfill contract.
Risk: If prices rise, must pay difference out of pocket (potentially bankrupting).
The Noble House Cornering
Major houses can manipulate markets legally:

House Ironroot: Corner iron market (prices ×3 for weapons).
House Silkveil: Control silk supply (affecting LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS.txt Tier 3+ clothing availability).
House Thorne: Military requisitions (seize all iron for war, civilian market dries up).
Player Counter-Manipulation:

Smuggling: Bring in goods from outside baronies (bypassing House control) for massive profit (Shadow Forge mechanics, +10 Detection risk).
Substitution: Promote alternative goods (linen instead of silk, bronze instead of iron) - requires DC 20 Administration and 3 months to shift market preferences.
4. COUNTERFEITING & FORGERY (The Shadow Forge Expanded)
The Shadow Forge Cartel specializes in perfect imitations that undermine legitimate economies.

The Counterfeiting Process
Step 1: Template Acquisition

Steal authentic item/document (DC 20 Stealth/Infiltration) or bribe insider (DC 18 Negotiation, 500 gp).
Examples: Noble house signet ring, Hammer & Anvil guild mark, Silkveil breeding pedigree, Alchemy reagent seal.
Step 2: Replication

Forged Documents: DC 22 Forgery (new skill under Crafting_System) or DC 24 Carving + Painting for seals/signets.
Counterfeit Goods: DC 25 Crafting (specific skill) + Deception (to mimic quality).
Time: 1 week per item complexity (simple letter) to 1 month (masterwork weapon with false pedigree).
Step 3: Distribution

Black Market: Sell at 50% of authentic price (buyers know it's fake but don't care).
Fraudulent Sale: Sell at 90% of authentic price (passing as real; DC 20 Deception vs. buyer's Appraisal).
Detection Mechanics
Visual Inspection (Casual): DC 12 (anyone) Expert Appraisal: DC 18 (Appraisal skill) to detect flaws. Magical Verification: DC 20 Arcana (Detect Magic reveals "forgery aura" on magical items). Guild Authentication: Hammer & Anvil has DC 25 verification process (microscopic maker's marks).

Consequences of Detection:

Buyer: Realizes fraud, demands refund (violent if refused; Combat_Rules.txt).
Authorities: Forgery is Moderate Crime (fines equal to item value ×2, or enslavement if unable to pay).
Noble Houses: Blood Grudge (Factions_and_Politics.txt) if you forge their seal; assassination attempts likely.
Specific Counterfeiting Applications
False Breeding Pedigrees (Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules.txt v1.11):

Forge bloodline documentation to sell common slave as "Verdant Grove-Born" (+200% price).
Risk: If discovered, buyer may demand return of offspring (if already bred), creating legal battle (Crime_Justice_System.txt Formal Trial).
Long-term: False bloodlines produce unstable traits (higher mutation chance when breeding).
Counterfeit Alchemical Reagents:

Sell colored sand as "Dragon Bile" (DC 16 Alchemy to detect).
Consequence: When used in potions, causes automatic Magic_Backlash_Table roll (explosive failure).
Ghost Slaves (Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt):

Forge manumission papers for slaves you don't own, "free" them, then re-enslave under new name.
Risk: If original owner discovers (DC 20 Investigation), you face Kidnapping (Major Crime) and the freed slave gains +40 Defiance (furious betrayal).
Estate Deeds (Property_Ownership.txt):

Forge ownership of abandoned mine or plantation.
Consequence: If true owner returns (or heir appears via Legacy_Tracker), violent property dispute (Mass Combat rules above).
The Master Forger (Prestige Path)
Shadow Forge Master (Tier 4+ Crafter Variant):

Can create Indistinguishable Forgeries (DC 30 required to detect; requires magical verification).
Signature Ability: "Ghost Signature" - forge documents that appear magically authentic (bypass Detect Magic) for 1 month, then degrade to obvious fakes.
Economic Impact: Can crash noble house economies by flooding market with perfect counterfeit luxury goods (destroying their monopoly value).

ECONOMY & TRADE SYSTEM - Version 1.26 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
The economy of the Shattered Principalities is built on scarcity, exploitation, and power. After 70 years since the Sundering War and the devastating 46 AE famine, trade is both the lifeblood of recovery and a constant source of tension. Slavery is not just social — it is the backbone of labor, agriculture, crafting, and pleasure industries. Wealth flows to those who control land, slaves, vehicles, workshops, and rare resources, but the system is volatile: raids, defiance uprisings, church scandals, noble rivalries, guild politics, and supply shocks can destroy fortunes overnight.
Crafting is a central pillar — turning raw materials and exploited labor into high-value goods that fuel trade, property upgrades, and erotic power displays. Multi-skill projects, guild/noble commissions, and black market operations create complex supply chains where success or failure ripples across the entire economy.
Currency
Gold Piece (gp) – Standard for major transactions, slave purchases, property, and high-end commissions.
Silver Piece (sp) = 1/10 gp – Everyday trade, wages, and workshop upkeep.
Copper Piece (cp) = 1/100 gp – Minor goods and basic slave maintenance.
Key Economic Pillars
Slavery & Labor
Slaves are valuable assets with market prices based on race, Body traits (Attractiveness, Scent, Hygiene, genital compatibility), training level, Defiance, and crafting skills.
Trained pleasure slaves or skilled artisans command premiums; bulk labor (orcs, goblins, undead) is cheaper but riskier.
Upkeep costs (food, clothing, hygiene supplies, restraints) are ongoing and scale with numbers and quality.
Property & Production
Land, workshops, farms, and estates generate passive income but require investment and maintenance.
Upgrades (playrooms, ritual chambers, reinforced slave quarters, dedicated crafting halls, herb gardens, alchemical labs) increase output and status.
Resource & Industrial properties (mines, lumber camps, mills, hunting cabins, roads) now form a dedicated production tier that supplies raw materials and creates new income streams (see Property_Ownership v1.7).
Crafting & Production Chains
Multi-skill projects turn raw materials into luxury, erotic, or enchanted goods.
Guild politics, noble commissions, and black market operations heavily influence pricing, availability, and profit margins.
Failures (poor craftsmanship, high Defiance sabotage, magical backlash) waste resources and damage reputation.
Trade & Vehicles
Caravans and ships move raw materials, finished crafted goods, and slaves between human baronies and racial settlements.
Profits depend on route danger, vehicle quality, crew/slave management, and market fluctuations.
Trade Road Segments / Toll Roads now provide direct passive toll income and improved Vehicle_System efficiency.
Pleasure & Erotic Economy
High-end courtesans, trained pleasure slaves, custom BDSM gear, and pleasure furniture are luxury goods.
Velvet Rose Society and Church of the Silken Collar heavily influence this sector.
Magic & Reagents
Rare reagents and enchanted items (often produced via magical crafting) are extremely valuable and volatile.
Mages and the Arcane Conclave control much of this black/grey market.
Black Market Crafting Trade
A parallel economy for forged, stolen, illegal, or experimental crafted goods that bypass guild monopolies and legal oversight.
Base Prices & Fluctuations
Prices are guidelines and shift based on supply/demand, recent events, faction/guild influence, noble commissions, and player actions.
Common Goods (food, basic tools, clothing): 1–15 gp
Luxury Goods (fine wine, high-quality restraints, perfumes): 25–150 gp
Crafted Items:
Simple (basic collar): 20–80 gp
Moderate (custom harness): 100–400 gp
Luxury/Erotic (pleasure throne, scent-infused lingerie): 300–2,000+ gp
Masterwork/Exotic (enchanted collar set, giant transport harness): 1,000–10,000+ gp
Slaves:
Basic labor (goblin, human): 50–250 gp
Skilled (dwarf craftsman, gnome tinker): 400–1,200 gp
Exotic pleasure (elf, dryad with high Attractiveness/Scent): 1,000–5,000+ gp
Giants: 3,000–15,000+ gp (logistical nightmare)
Market Fluctuation Tables
Roll on these tables periodically (once per major trade run, per season, or when narrative demands). Add +2 to the roll on dangerous routes, during scandals, or after major player actions that disrupt supply. Black market activity adds another +2 and increases chance of Chaotic results.
Stable Markets (d20) – Normal conditions
1–8 Steady: No change.
9–12 Minor surplus: Prices drop 10–20% for common goods or basic slaves.
13–16 Minor shortage: Prices rise 10–25% for luxury goods or skilled slaves.
17–19 Opportunity: Temporary buyer for high-value pleasure slaves or enchanted items (+20–50% profit on one sale).
20 Boom: One category surges (+30–50% profit potential).
Volatile Markets (d20) – Common after raids, scandals, or player interference
1–4 Glut: Prices crash 25–40% for one major category (e.g., labor slaves or grain).
5–8 Shortage: Prices spike 30–60% for one category (e.g., reagents or exotic dryads).
9–12 Faction interference: Iron Collar or Verdant Consortium manipulation causes artificial price swing (±30%).
13–15 Scandal ripple: Church or noble scandal affects luxury/pleasure trade (–20% or +40% depending on faction).
16–18 Raid aftermath: Recent Freeborn or bandit activity disrupts routes (–25% profit or lost cargo).
19–20 Major event: Famine echo, church edict, or noble war causes wild swings across multiple categories.
Chaotic Markets (d20) – Rare, triggered by major events or player actions
1–3 Market crash: All slave and luxury prices drop 40–70%. Mass panic selling.
4–6 Boom & Bust: One category explodes (+100%+) while another collapses (–60%).
7–9 Church intervention: Silken Collar blessing or Broken Chain condemnation shifts pleasure/slavery prices dramatically.
10–12 Noble house war: Rival houses embargo or flood markets, causing chaos for 1–2 months.
13–15 Magical disruption: Arcane Conclave action or rogue ritual makes reagents unpredictable (±50% wild swings).
16–18 Mass defiance/uprising: Slave labor becomes unreliable; prices for trained slaves spike while output drops.
19–20 Catastrophe: Major raid, natural disaster, or scandal wipes out a trade route or entire market sector (potential total loss).
Expanded Market Fluctuation Examples (Tied to Resource & Industrial Properties)
Owning a Modest Mine Shaft during a metal shortage: Iron and copper prices spike +60%, giving owners a massive profit window when selling raw ore or crafted goods.
Established Lumber Camp during a building boom: Processed lumber prices rise +40–70%; owners can sell directly to noble house construction projects or vehicle makers.
Remote Hunting / Trapping Cabin during winter: Fur and hide prices surge +50% due to cold-weather demand, but monster attacks increase.
Windmill or Watermill during a grain famine echo: Flour and bread prices skyrocket +80%, but weather-dependent mills may fail and cause local unrest.
Trade Road Segment / Toll Road during heavy caravan season: Toll income can double, but bandit activity and rival house interference also increase.
New Resource & Industrial Property Fluctuation Table (d20)
Roll once per month if the player owns any Resource & Industrial property:
1–5 Steady production: Normal income, no major events.
6–9 Surplus output: +20–40% income this month from raw materials.
10–12 Supply disruption (cave-in, fire, storm): –30% income and +10–20 Defiance for on-site slaves.
13–15 High demand spike: +50–80% profit on one raw material type (e.g., ore, lumber, hides).
16–18 Faction or noble interest: A house or guild offers a bulk purchase contract (potential +100% profit but locks in future supply).
19–20 Major event: Raid, church protest, or rival claim – possible loss of property or forced sale at reduced price.

ESTATE_MANAGEMENT_AND_DAILY_LIFE - Version 1.6 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
Owning property in the Shattered Principalities is never passive wealth — it is a constant burden of management, exploitation, and risk. Estates (farms, workshops, mines, lumber camps, hunting lodges, pleasure houses, etc.) are the foundation of power, but they require daily oversight, slave labor coordination, resource balancing, and maintenance. Neglect leads to Defiance spikes, economic loss, sabotage, scandals, or outright rebellion. Successful management turns raw land and exploited labor into steady income, upgraded holdings, and erotic prestige, but every choice carries grimdark weight: overwork breeds resentment, luxury invites envy, and magical shortcuts invite backlash.
This file provides the daily, weekly, and long-term framework for estate life. It integrates directly with Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt (Defiance drift from routines), Property_Ownership.txt (upgrades and facilities), Economy_Trade_System.txt (income and market fluctuations), Crafting_System.txt (workshop output), Vehicle_System.txt (transport logistics), Wilderness_Areas.txt (resource gathering and festival effects), and Time_System.txt v1.1 (daily structure, seasonal effects, aging consequences, and long-term generational management).
Estate Types & Base Characteristics
Estates are categorized by primary function. Each starts at a basic level and can be upgraded via Property_Ownership rules.
Agricultural Plantation (Verdant March style): Focus on crops, herbs, aphrodisiacs. High slave numbers, seasonal cycles.
Mining / Quarry Operation (Ironvein style): Heavy labor, dangerous conditions, high Defiance risk.
Workshop / Forge Complex (Hammer & Anvil style): Crafting focus, multi-skill projects, tool-dependent output.
Lumber / Hunting Camp (Bloodthorn / wilderness style): Resource gathering, mobile labor, high wilderness interaction.
Pleasure Estate / Brothel (Silken Reach style): Erotic focus, high Scent/Hygiene demands, reputation-sensitive.
Mixed or Custom Estate: Combination of above; requires balanced slave assignment.
Daily Estate Routine Framework
A typical day is divided into four blocks that align with the Time_System.txt v1.1 phases (Morning, Midday, Evening, Night). The owner (or assigned overseers) must allocate slaves, time, and resources each morning. Neglect or poor allocation in any block causes automatic Defiance drift. All blocks are further modified by seasonal effects and aging from Time_System v1.1.
Labor Block (Morning – 6–12 hours, adjustable by season/weather): Primary work shift. Assigned tasks determine base output and Defiance modifiers (see Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics daily factors). Physical labor, crafting, resource extraction, or breeding-related duties occur here. Aging and seasonal time costs from Time_System apply directly. Overwork or dangerous conditions (mines, lumber) amplify Defiance growth.
Maintenance Block (Midday): Critical hygiene, feeding, medical care, and upkeep. Includes bathing, meals, clothing/footwear cleaning and repair, and basic medical attention. Clothing and footwear maintenance is explicitly required here — neglected or damaged luxury garments or high-heeled footwear cause rapid Hygiene degradation, Scent penalties, and additional Defiance spikes. Aging-related upkeep (longevity potions, alchemical treatments) can also be scheduled in this block.
Oversight / Motivation Block (Evening): Owner or overseer interaction time. Includes BDSM motivation scenes, training, inspections, public displays, or erotic reward sessions. This is the primary window for reducing daily Defiance through kink-matched scenes and proper aftercare.
Recovery / Night Block (Night): Sleep, overnight security, and passive recovery. Poor conditions or inadequate security add +2 to +5 Defiance. Monthly Aging Checks (Time_System v1.1) and long-term generational tracking are resolved at the end of each month during this phase.
Weekly & Monthly Cycles
Weekly Review: Roll for random events (link to Random_Events_Table or estate-specific sub-table). Calculate income, resource consumption, and cumulative Defiance drift. Apply seasonal modifiers from Time_System.
Monthly Accounting: Pay upkeep, sell surplus goods, handle breeding outcomes, apply long-term upgrades or repairs, and perform the Monthly Aging Check for the owner, key NPCs, and significant slaves (Time_System v1.1). Major festivals or raids can interrupt this cycle.
Resource & Labor Management
Slave Assignment: Divide labor pool across tasks. High-Defiance slaves reduce output by 20–50% and increase sabotage risk. Aging slaves (especially in labor-heavy estates) suffer reduced productivity and higher injury risk.
Resource Tracking: Food, tools, raw materials, and luxury goods (scented oils, restraints) must be balanced. Shortages cause Defiance spikes; surpluses can be used for motivation or trade. Clothing and footwear upkeep is now a tracked resource — luxury or high-heeled items require dedicated maintenance to retain their attractiveness bonuses. Longevity potions and alchemical aging treatments are additional monthly resources with high cost and backlash risk.
Output Calculation: Base output modified by average slave skill, Defiance level, workshop upgrades, racial bonuses, and aging effects (older slaves lose productivity unless maintained with alchemy/magic).
Estate Upkeep & Income
Base weekly upkeep scales with size and luxury level. Income comes from:

Surplus goods sold via Economy_Trade_System.
Crafted items (Crafting_System).
Pleasure services or breeding contracts.
Resource extraction (wilderness gathering or mining).

Passive Defiance Drift Table (per day, before motivation scenes)

Well-maintained estate with balanced routines: –2 to –6 group average.
Overworked or neglected estate: +4 to +12 group average.
Luxury pleasure estate with poor hygiene: +6 to +15 (Scent scandals common).
Festival aftermath: Special one-time modifiers (see Wilderness_Areas).
Aging owner or key overseer decline (from Time_System): +2 to +8 additional drift due to perceived weakness.

Magic_System Integration
Alteration or Enchanting rituals can temporarily boost output or suppress Defiance (–10 to –20) but add backlash risk that may cause sudden spikes, mutations, or summoning events during daily routines.
Necromantic labor (undead slaves) reduces Defiance issues but risks church scandals and permanent corruption bleed.
Ritual chambers on the estate (Property_Ownership upgrade) allow daily minor rituals but increase detection risk from rival factions. Aging-related rituals (longevity or generational stabilization) are particularly powerful here but carry severe backlash that can affect the entire estate.
Faction Politics & Estate Management

Broken Chain: Views poor conditions as evidence of sin; high Defiance can trigger reformist investigations or public denunciations.
Verdant Cycle: Prefers natural, sustainable estates; over-exploitation in wilderness areas may bring dryad protests or curses.
Hammer & Anvil: Rewards efficient workshops; sabotage from high Defiance can lead to guild blacklisting.
Freeborn League: High-Defiance estates in border zones become prime targets for raids or propaganda.
