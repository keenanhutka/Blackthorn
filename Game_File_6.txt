FACTION_REPUTATION_SYSTEM - Version 1.5 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)

Core Philosophy
Reputation is the currency of power in the Shattered Principalities. In a world rebuilt on slavery, betrayal, and erotic dominance, how factions perceive you determines everything: prices at auction, quest availability, legal protection, access to forbidden knowledge, and even whether you wake up with a knife in your back or an invitation to a noble orgy. Reputation is tracked separately for each major faction and can shift dramatically from a single well-handled (or disastrously public) BDSM scene, slave auction, trade deal, or magical ritual.

Reputation Scale (per faction)
Track reputation numerically from -100 to +100 for fine-grained play, but use these public-facing tiers for narrative and mechanical effects:

- Hostile (-100 to -51): Open enemies. Faction will actively sabotage, arrest, raid, or assassinate you. Prices +100%, no quests, constant risk of attack or scandal.
- Unfriendly (-50 to -1): Distrustful. Harsh prices (+50%), limited or poisoned quests, higher legal scrutiny, and social snubs. Public displays of ownership can trigger immediate backlash.
- Neutral (0 to +50): Business as usual. Standard prices and basic access. No special favors or hostility.
- Friendly (+51 to +80): Trusted ally. Discounted prices (-25%), priority quests, legal leniency, and invitations to private events (including erotic faction gatherings). Public BDSM displays or “gifting” well-trained slaves can raise reputation quickly.
- Ally (+81 to +100): Inner circle. Major discounts (-50%), exclusive quests, protection from rival factions, and access to secret resources (forbidden magic, high-end pleasure slaves, ritual chambers). Faction may offer political marriages, joint ventures, or personal favors.

Reputation Actions – How to Gain or Lose It
Actions have different impact depending on the faction’s goals and values. Public or witnessed actions have double effect.

Major Reputation Gains (+5 to +30)
- Successful kink-matched public or private BDSM scenes that align with faction tastes (e.g., Silken Collar loves decadent displays; Verdant Cycle favors gentle nature play).
- Generous gifting of high-Attractiveness, low-Hygiene, high-Sexual-Corruption slaves with matching Body traits and kinks.
- High-quality crafted items or vehicles commissioned for the faction (especially Engineering or erotic luxury goods).
- Protecting faction interests (raiding rivals, suppressing rebellions, discreetly handling scandals).
- Public ownership displays that reinforce the faction’s ideology (e.g., pro-slavery pageantry for Silken Collar).

Major Reputation Losses (-5 to -30)
- Poor slave treatment, high Defiance outbreaks, or visible neglect (especially if it causes scandals).
- Using banned magic (Necromancy with Broken Chain, unauthorized Conjuration anywhere).
- Betraying faction secrets, failing commissions, or being caught in rival alliances.
- Public BDSM scenes that contradict faction values (e.g., gentle aftercare in front of Bloodvein traditionalists).
- High-profile failures (slave escape during travel, botched ritual, counterfeit goods).

Faction-Specific Reputation Notes
- Iron Collar Guild: Values professional slave handling and profitable auctions. High Penile/Flagellation/Bondage skills impress them.
- Verdant Consortium: Rewards gentle but firm ownership of “natural” slaves (Dryads, elves). High Herbalism and Aftercare skills help.
- Silken Collar Church: Loves public decadent displays and high Sexual Corruption. Erotic Performance skills and public scenes are huge reputation boosters.
- Broken Chain Church: Rewards humane treatment and low Corruption. Gentle scenes and high Aftercare can raise reputation (but they remain suspicious of heavy BDSM).
- Noble Houses: Reputation is personal and cutthroat. House Silkveil favors luxurious erotic gifts; House Bloodvein respects brutal efficiency; House Whisperveil values discretion and blackmail material.
- Black Market Factions: Reputation is earned through successful smuggling runs, counterfeit crafting (Engineering), and discreet erotic services.

Mechanical Effects by Tier
- Prices: Hostile +100%, Unfriendly +50%, Neutral standard, Friendly -25%, Ally -50%.
- Quest Access: Hostile = none or trap quests. Ally = exclusive high-reward quests (including rare magical or erotic commissions).
- Legal Protection: Hostile = automatic investigations or bounties. Ally = faction lawyers or guards intervene on your behalf.
- Slave Market & Trade: Higher reputation unlocks better slaves, bulk discounts, and private auctions.
- BDSM & Social Scenes: Ally status can turn public scenes into reputation-boosting spectacles; Hostile status can turn them into scandals.
- Magic & Crafting: Ally factions may provide rare reagents or ritual spaces; Hostile factions may sabotage or steal enchanted items.
