TIMELINE - Version 1.2 (70 AE)

The Sundering War & Its Aftermath
The Sundering War was a cataclysmic conflict that shattered the previous multi-racial empires. It lasted roughly a decade and ended in mutual exhaustion, leaving behind ruined cities, cursed battlefields, and a generation scarred by loss. The war accelerated existing racial tensions and created massive refugee movements.

0 AE – The Sundering War Ends
- The great empires collapse. Borders dissolve into countless small baronies and independent racial settlements.
- Massive displacement creates the first wave of debt bondage as survivors sell themselves or their families for food and protection.

20–45 AE – Reconstruction Period
- Surviving noble houses and guilds begin rebuilding trade routes and workshops.
- Early forms of regulated slavery emerge, primarily as debt repayment.
- Hammer & Anvil Guild forms as a coalition of craftsmen seeking to protect their monopolies.
- The three churches begin to solidify their doctrines around ownership and morality.

46 AE – The Great Famine
- A combination of poor harvests, lingering magical corruption from the war, and disrupted trade routes causes widespread starvation across the region.
- Millions die or are displaced. Desperate populations sign multi-generational debt contracts in exchange for food and protection.
- These “famine contracts” become the legal and cultural foundation of modern hereditary slavery.
- Racial animosity spikes as groups blame each other for hoarding resources.
- The Iron Collar Guild rises to prominence by organizing slave markets and “re-education” services.
- Erotic power dynamics shift dramatically: pleasure slaves become a luxury commodity for the wealthy while public displays of submission are used to demonstrate control over scarce resources.
- The Church of the Silken Collar gains massive influence by framing ownership and erotic submission as divine gifts that bring order amid chaos.
- The Order of the Broken Chain begins as a small reformist movement decrying the moral horror of famine-era bondage.

47–60 AE – Recovery & Entrenchment
- Surviving baronies stabilize under noble houses (Voss, Thorne, Silkveil, etc.).
- Slavery becomes institutionalized and economically essential. Multi-generational contracts are upheld by law.
- Crafting guilds (especially Hammer & Anvil) recover and begin producing specialized restraints, vehicles, and luxury erotic items.
- Black market networks (precursors to Shadow Forge and Crimson Veil) emerge to supply forbidden goods.
- Racial settlements re-form: elves and dryads in forest groves, orcs in rugged borderlands, dwarves in mountain holds, etc.
- The three churches solidify their rivalries:
  - Silken Collar becomes the faith of decadent nobles and pleasure houses.
  - Broken Chain grows among the poor and former debt slaves.
  - Verdant Cycle maintains influence in nature-aligned communities.

61–70 AE – Current Era (The Present)
- Blackthorn Vale and Thornhaven emerge as a key trade and political hub.
- Noble houses compete fiercely through crafting commissions, erotic diplomacy, and strategic slave ownership.
- Black market factions (Shadow Forge Cartel, Crimson Veil Syndicate, Bone & Blood Collective, Whispered Ledger) operate openly in the shadows, supplying counterfeit luxury goods and forbidden magical items.
- Rival crafting factions (Iron Vein Collective, Velvet Hammer, Shadow Forge) create constant tension within the Hammer & Anvil Guild.
- Slavery remains the economic backbone, but defiance uprisings and Freeborn League activity are growing threats.
- Erotic power is more visible than ever: public BDSM ceremonies, custom pleasure slaves, and lavish “gifting” rituals are standard tools of alliance-building and status display.
- Hygiene and Scent management have become markers of competence — neglected slaves signal poor ownership and damage reputation.
- Magic remains rare and dangerous, but enchanted restraints and alchemical enhancements are highly prized luxury items.

Key Lasting Impacts of the 46 AE Famine
- Multi-generational slavery is culturally and legally normalized. Many slaves today are descendants of famine-era contracts.
- Racial animosity is uneven: some areas are relatively cosmopolitan while others enforce strict hierarchies or segregation.
- Economic volatility remains high — famine echoes still cause periodic shortages that spike slave prices and trigger defiance waves.
- The churches gained massive influence during the crisis and continue to shape moral debates around ownership and erotic practice.
- Crafted luxury goods (especially erotic items) became status symbols as survivors sought comfort and control amid scarcity.

Current Year: 70 AE
The region is in a fragile equilibrium. Ambitious individuals can rise quickly through clever ownership, masterful erotic diplomacy, high-quality crafting, or strategic black market dealings — but one bad harvest, slave uprising, church scandal, or noble betrayal can destroy everything.

This timeline provides the historical foundation for all mechanics and stories in the campaign.

TIME_SYSTEM - Version 1.1 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE - Shattered Principalities)

Core Philosophy
Time in the Shattered Principalities is harsh, consequential, and unforgiving. Days pass quickly under the weight of labor, training, breeding, and survival. Characters and NPCs age realistically, with physical and social consequences that reflect the grimdark reality of exploitation, pregnancy, hard labor, and hedonistic excess. Time mechanics integrate directly with Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life, Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics, Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules, Social_Advancement, and Random_Events_Table to create meaningful long-term progression and risk.

All time is tracked in discrete units to keep play manageable while allowing meaningful passage of weeks, months, and years.

Time Units & Actions
- Round: 6 seconds (combat or intense scenes)
- Minute: 10 rounds (quick tasks)
- Hour: 60 minutes (typical work shift or training session)
- Day: 24 hours (default unit for daily routines)
- Week: 7 days (estate management cycle)
- Month: 30 days (breeding cycles, market fluctuations, aging checks)
- Year: 12 months (major aging, generational changes, campaign milestones)

Daily Time Structure (recommended for estate-focused play)
A standard day is divided into 4 phases. Players assign actions to phases. Slaves and followers require oversight or risk Defiance spikes.
- Morning (Labor / Training): Physical work, crafting, combat training, or BDSM motivation scenes.
- Midday (Maintenance / Business): Estate upkeep, trade, fashion/hygiene maintenance, public displays.
- Evening (Social / Pleasure): Negotiation, faction meetings, erotic scenes, festivals.
- Night (Rest / Risk): Sleep, guard duty, or secret activities. Poor rest increases Defiance drift.

Action Time Costs (examples)
- Basic slave training session: 1–2 hours (BDSM_System)
- Crafting a Tier 2 restraint: 4–8 hours (Crafting_System)
- Full daily estate oversight: 4–6 hours (Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life)
- Public fashion display or negotiation: 1–3 hours (LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS + Social_Advancement)
- Breeding attempt or ritual: 1–4 hours (Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules + Magic_System)
- Travel day (normal pace): 8–10 hours movement + rest
- Major festival or public event: Full day (Random_Events_Table)

Aging Mechanics
Aging affects Body traits, fertility, endurance, and social perception. All characters and NPCs age unless magically altered.

Aging Schedule
- Human baseline: Prime years 18–35. Visible aging begins at 36. Significant decline after 50.
- Elf/Dryad: Slower aging (prime until ~150, visible aging ~200+). Elves and Dryads retain youthful appearance longer but suffer sudden, dramatic decline once aging begins (–2 to multiple traits in one check after 200).
- Orc: Faster physical peak and decline (prime 15–40, decline after 45). Orcs gain +1 Strength/Endurance in prime but lose –2 to both traits rapidly after 45. Fertility window is short but intense.
- Giant: Very slow maturation but rapid wear (prime 20–60, visible aging after 65). Giants suffer massive –3 to Strength and Endurance on failed aging checks due to joint strain and size-related stress. Social perception of “aging giant” often triggers fear or pity.
- Goblin: Extremely fast aging (prime 10–25, decline after 30). Goblins gain quick skill bonuses early but suffer –1 to all physical traits every 2–3 months after 30. High risk of early death or permanent disability.
- Other races: Use Races_Reference.txt for baseline maturity and aging rates. Mixed-race offspring use the slower parent’s rate as baseline but inherit accelerated decline traits from the faster-aging parent.

Monthly Aging Check (at end of each month)
Roll 1d20 + Constitution modifier (or relevant racial trait).
- Natural 20 or higher: No aging effects this month. Possible minor positive trait shift (e.g., +1 Attractiveness from disciplined life).
- 11–19: Normal aging. No mechanical change.
- 6–10: Minor aging. –1 to one physical Body trait (Strength, Endurance, or Attractiveness). Fertility reduced by 5% if applicable.
- 2–5: Noticeable aging. –2 to one physical trait, –1 to another. Social rolls involving beauty or vitality suffer –1. Defiance drift from slaves increases +2 if owner appears weakened.
- Natural 1: Major aging event. –3 to two physical traits, possible mutation or chronic condition. Reputation bleed with factions that value youth/beauty. High risk of slave Defiance spike due to perceived weakness.

Racial Aging Modifiers (applied to the Monthly Aging Check)
- Elf/Dryad: –4 to the roll (aging is rare but severe when it happens).
- Orc: +2 to the roll (faster decline).
- Giant: +1 to the roll but any failure of 10 or lower causes double trait loss due to size strain.
- Goblin: +5 to the roll (very rapid decline).
- Mixed heritage: Average the modifiers or use the more severe one for dramatic effect.

Pregnancy & Breeding Aging Effects (Pregnancy_Breeding_Rules integration)
- Successful pregnancy accelerates visible aging for the bearer (+1 month equivalent per trimester).
- Multiple births or magical augmentation can cause permanent Body trait shifts (e.g., increased breast size but reduced endurance).
- Generational aging: Offspring inherit blended traits and begin their own aging clock at birth.

Seasonal Time Effects
Seasons influence daily time costs, aging checks, and Defiance drift. Apply these every season (every 3 months).

Spring (Fertility & Growth)
- Breeding and training actions take –1 hour (renewed energy).
- Aging checks gain –2 to the roll (growth feels hopeful).
- +5 daily Defiance drift reduction for slaves in nature-infused or well-maintained fashion.

Summer (Heat & Oppression)
- All physical labor and travel actions take +1–2 hours (heat exhaustion).
- Aging checks gain +2 to the roll (accelerated wear).
- +10 daily Defiance drift for all slaves. Luxury fashion (Tier 3+) helps but risks heat-related scent degradation.

Autumn (Harvest & Decay)
- Crafting and trade actions gain –10% time cost (focused productivity).
- Aging checks gain +1 to the roll (decay sets in).
- Luxury goods and fashion sell at premium but maintenance costs rise. Scent/perfume effectiveness peaks.

Winter (Cold & Desperation)
- All outdoor actions take +2–3 hours (cold slows movement).
- Aging checks gain +3 to the roll (harsh survival).
- Heavy warm fashion (Tier 3+) reduces Defiance drift by –8 but limits activity. Poorly clothed slaves suffer +15 daily Defiance drift and faster aging.

Long-Term Consequences
- Owners over 50 with poor maintenance or heavy breeding schedules risk social demotion (Social_Advancement penalties).
- High-tier luxury fashion and alchemical aids (Alchemy_Consumables_and_Potions) can delay visible aging by 1–2 years per successful regimen but carry corruption or backlash risk.
- Slaves with high Sexual Corruption may age more slowly in appearance but suffer faster physical decline from overuse.

Integration with Core Systems
- Daily/weekly time feeds directly into Estate_Management_and_Daily_Life and Random_Events_Table.
- Aging checks interact with Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics (Defiance drift increases with owner weakness) and LUXURY_FASHION_AND_STATUS (fashion can mask aging but poor maintenance reveals it dramatically).
- Magic_System rituals or potions can slow or reverse aging at high cost and risk.
- Social_Advancement rewards long-term success but penalizes visible decline.
- Seasonal effects tie directly into Random_Events_Table modifiers and estate routines.

Future Expansion Notes
Seasonal or festival time compression.
Detailed generational family trees.
Magical longevity options with grimdark costs.

We will expand this system together. Suggest refinements or additional mechanics anytime.

VEHICLE SYSTEM - Version 1.10 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Philosophy
Vehicles are essential for trade, slave transport, military movement, and smuggling in the Shattered Principalities. From simple horse-drawn wagons to massive airships, vehicles represent significant investments that can generate wealth or lead to ruin. In this grimdark world, travel is rarely safe — cramped conditions amplify Scent and Hygiene issues, high-Defiance slaves increase escape risk, and valuable cargo (especially black market crafted goods or pleasure slaves) attracts bandits, rival factions, and church inquisitors.
Vehicles tie directly into Economy & Trade (cargo capacity and profit), Crafting (custom parts and upgrades), Slavery & Defiance (transport security), Sex/BDSM (private compartments for scenes), and black market operations (hidden smuggling features). Magical vehicles are extremely rare, expensive, and dangerous, requiring rituals from the Magic_System and carrying high backlash risk.

Slave-Drawn Mechanics (Expanded)
Slaves can replace beasts on any beast-drawn vehicle (or on water galleys). This is cheaper but far riskier and carries heavy grimdark consequences. Slave-drawn vehicles are common in backwards enclaves and among brutal factions but are viewed as barbaric by reformist churches.
Daily Defiance Growth: +8–15 per slave pulling (higher than normal labor due to exhaustion, humiliation, and physical strain). Orcs and Giants reduce this by 4 due to natural strength; smaller races (Goblins, Gnomes) increase it by +5; Dryads and Elves suffer an extra +3 due to delicate physiology.
Mixed Teams: A combination of beasts and slaves is possible. Each slave in the team adds +6–12 Defiance growth but reduces overall Animal Handling DC by 2 (slaves can be motivated more directly than animals). This is common on mines or lumber camps where owners use both for efficiency.
Operation Check: d20 + lowest slave’s Endurance or Strength modifier (or Animal Handling if beasts are present) vs. DC 15–20. Failure reduces speed by 50% and adds +5–10 Defiance to all pulling slaves that day. Critical failure causes injury (permanent –5 to Endurance for 1 week or longer).
Mitigation: Successful BDSM motivation/reward scenes (using Domination or Flagellation) grant advantage on the daily check and reduce Defiance growth by half for that day. High Sexual Corruption slaves tolerate pulling better and may even gain minor Pleasure Points from the exertion (turning humiliation into a kink). Public “motivation” scenes on the road can also grant faction reputation with brutal houses.
Long-term risks: Overuse (more than 3 consecutive days) causes permanent injury, rebellion risk (+20 Defiance spike), or church scandals (especially Broken Chain and Verdant Cycle). Public slave-drawn vehicles lower reputation with reformist factions but raise it with brutal ones (House Bloodvein, House Thorne). After 7+ days of continuous pulling, slaves gain a permanent +10 Defiance floor unless exceptional aftercare is provided.
Industrial Synergy: Slave-drawn carts at Modest Mine Shafts or Established Lumber Camps gain +2 to operation checks (familiar terrain) but increase daily Defiance growth by +5 due to heavy loads.
Integration: Ties directly to Slavery & Defiance (high daily checks), Sex/BDSM (motivation scenes become mandatory), Property_Ownership (slave pens on vehicles reduce penalties), and Factions (public use triggers Reputation Events).

Expanded Magical Backlash Mechanics
Magical vehicles require ongoing ritual maintenance. Each week (or after any major event), the owner makes a Magic_System ritual check (DC 22–30 depending on vehicle power).
Critical Success: Vehicle gains temporary bonus (e.g., +20% speed or +3 to BDSM scenes for one journey).
Success: Normal operation.
Failure by 5 or less: Minor backlash (temporary loss of one feature, +10 Defiance to all aboard, or minor mutation on slaves).
Failure by 6+: Major backlash (vehicle crippled for 1d4 days, random Magical Event triggered, possible crash/strand, or permanent curse such as “haunted” compartments that cause nightmares).
Natural 1: Catastrophic backlash (vehicle destroyed or teleported to dangerous location; all aboard suffer mutations, soul corruption, or summoning of hostile entities).
Church/Arcane Note: Backlash on magical vehicles often triggers immediate Faction Reputation loss or inquisitor pursuit.

4–6 Magical Backlash: Enchanted cargo activates unexpectedly (especially dangerous on magical vehicles).
7–9 Noble House Interference: Specific house intervenes based on cargo type.
10–12 Mass Escape: Slaves break free and flee.
13–15 Environmental Catastrophe: Bridge collapse or monster attack (or storm/reef for water vehicles; planar rift for magical vehicles).
16–18 Church Crusade: Mobile inquisitors target forbidden items.
19–20 Windfall with Price: Discover rival cache but gain heavy Detection risk.
Faction Reactions During Travel
Silken Collar: Approves discreet luxury travel; may bless vehicles.
Broken Chain: Condemns overcrowded slave transport; may aid escapes.
Hammer & Anvil / Ironroot: Favor well-crafted vehicles; sabotage poorly maintained ones.
Shadow Forge / Black Market Factions: Offer smuggling routes but demand cuts.
Arcane Conclave: Intrigued by magical vehicles; may demand samples or ritual access.

SLAVERY_AND_DEFIANCE_CORE_MECHANICS INTEGRATION (New Section)
Vehicle travel is one of the highest daily sources of Defiance growth and rebellion risk in Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics.txt v1.8. Cramped conditions, slave-draught labor, and transport security directly feed into daily Defiance change factors and rebellion chains.
Key Mechanical Ties:
- Daily Defiance Change Factors during travel are heavily amplified: cramped conditions add +5–15 Defiance per slave per day (higher on slave-drawn or water galleys). Slave-draught mechanics now explicitly use the Daily Defiance Growth rules (+8–15 per pulling slave, modified by race and Sexual Corruption).
- BDSM Motivation Scenes become mandatory during long journeys. A successful scene (especially Reward & Bonding or Group Motivation) during the Oversight Block of estate routines (when staging from a property) or mid-travel can halve Defiance growth for that day and grant advantage on Operation Checks.
- Rebellion & Escape Mechanics are triggered far more easily during travel: high-Defiance slaves on vehicles have increased chance of Escape Probe or Partial Escape Attempt stages. Critical failures on Operation Checks or Security Checks can immediately escalate into a Moderate or Major Rebellion Chain.
- Aftercare Rules are critical after any travel-related motivation scene; failure adds +3 to +8 Defiance the next day and increases the chance of vehicle-related incidents (injury, scent leak, or sabotage).
- Festival Rebellion Examples and Racial Scene Nuances apply strongly during travel: poor performance in an Orc or Giant Breeding Festival before a journey can cause immediate Defiance spikes that make slave-drawn or cramped travel extremely dangerous.
- Breast Breeding & Induced Lactation during travel adds new risks: overmilking in cramped conditions causes soreness and Defiance spikes, while proper milking scenes can provide temporary Defiance reduction but risk public scandals or Hygiene crises.
- Generational Defiance Inheritance is affected by travel during pregnancy: harsh transport conditions raise inherited Defiance floors for offspring.

Specific Interactions:
- Slave-Drawn Vehicles: Daily Operation Check failure now directly feeds into Rebellion Resolution Tables. Overuse (more than 3 days) causes permanent +10 Defiance floor unless exceptional aftercare is provided.
- Beast vs Slave Draught: Beast-drawn is safer for Defiance but requires Animal Handling; slave-drawn is cheaper but risks rapid escalation into rebellion chains unless mitigated by frequent BDSM motivation scenes.
- Magical Vehicles: Backlash during travel can cause sudden Defiance spikes (+10–30) or mutations that trigger Major Rebellion stages.
- Estate Security & Overseer Loyalty: Vehicles departing from well-managed estates with loyal overseers and active patrols gain –3 to –8 on travel event rolls and reduced Defiance growth.
- Water Vehicles & Galleys: Slave rowing adds extreme Defiance growth (+12–25 daily); capsizing or boarding events can instantly trigger Mass Escape stages.

Integration with Other Systems
Crafting_System: Custom vehicle parts, restraints, and compartments are produced via multi-skill projects. New water vehicles can be upgraded with oar benches or reinforced hulls at Sawmills or Watermills. Magical vehicles require multi-skill + ritual projects. Parts and gear are drawn from Combat_Equipment.txt, BDSM_Restraints_and_Pleasure_Gear.txt, and Raw_Materials_Tools_Trade_Goods.txt.
Economy_Trade_System: Vehicles enable bulk trade runs; smuggling events directly tie into black market profits and risks. New water vehicles benefit from trade roads and mills near rivers. Magical vehicles allow premium long-distance routes with higher profit but greater risk.
Slavery_and_Defiance_Core_Mechanics: Transporting high-Defiance slaves increases escape risk and Defiance spikes in cramped conditions (especially on barges and galleys). Magical vehicles can reduce Defiance via enchanted restraints but risk backlash mutations. Slave-drafted vehicles dramatically increase Defiance unless mitigated by BDSM scenes.
Sex/BDSM_System: Private compartments allow mid-travel scenes; cramped conditions amplify Scent/Hygiene effects and kink opportunities. Luxury cabins on airships and magical vehicles grant +3 to +5 bonuses (see BDSM_Restraints_and_Pleasure_Gear.txt).
Magic_System: Magical vehicles require ongoing rituals and carry high backlash risk. Backlash on an airship or rift skiff can be catastrophic.
Factions_and_Politics: Vehicle quality and travel success affect reputation with guilds, noble houses, and churches. Magical vehicles attract Arcane Conclave attention and church scrutiny. Public slave-drawn vehicles trigger major reputation swings.
Edge Cases
Giant Transport: Requires specialized giant haulers; failure can destroy the vehicle or injure giants.
Black Market Cargo: Hidden compartments reduce detection but increase breakdown risk.
Public vs Private Travel: Luxurious carriages raise status but attract attention; plain wagons are safer but less comfortable for erotic scenes.
Water Vehicles: River barges and galleys have higher risk of capsizing or boarding in bad weather; slave rowers increase Defiance growth dramatically.
Magical Vehicles: Extreme cost and backlash make them status symbols for the very wealthy; losing one can bankrupt a player or trigger noble house investigations.
Beast vs Slave Draught: Beast-drawn is safer for Defiance but requires animal upkeep; slave-drawn is cheaper but risks rebellion unless actively managed with BDSM scenes.
