SESSION_0_QUICKSTART_GUIDE - Version 1.3 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)

Core Philosophy
Session 0 is the foundation of a successful grimdark erotic campaign in the Shattered Principalities. It sets expectations for the dark tone, heavy consequences, adult content boundaries, and collaborative storytelling. The goal is to ensure every player understands that power is intoxicating but fragile, pleasure carries risk, and every choice has lasting repercussions (Defiance spikes, scandals, mutations, faction grudges, etc.).

How to Run Session 0 (60–90 minutes recommended)
1. Tone & Safety Discussion (10–15 min)
   - Confirm all players are 18+ and comfortable with grimdark themes: slavery, non-consensual elements (within Defiance/BDSM mechanics), erotic violence, breeding, corruption, betrayal, and moral gray areas.
   - Establish hard limits and soft limits using a simple checklist (e.g., no underage content, no certain kinks).
   - Emphasize the Adult trait gate — erotic, breeding, or BDSM mechanics only apply to Adult characters.
   - Remind everyone that the game is consequence-heavy: failed Defiance rolls, public scandals, church scrutiny, and backlash are expected and fun.

2. Character Creation Walkthrough (20–30 min)
   - Use Player_Character.txt v1.6 as the primary guide.
   - Step through each section together:
     - Race + Racial Feat choice
     - Ability Scores
     - Background & Social Standing
     - Class Archetype (highlight synergies)
     - Skill ranks (120 total, with archetype recommendations)
     - Body Traits (use Body_Ranges_Reference.txt)
     - Sexual Corruption & starting Defiance (if applicable)
     - Starting Equipment Package

SETTING_OVERVIEW - Version 1.5 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)
Core Setting
The world is known as the Shattered Principalities — a fractured region once dominated by grand multi-racial empires that collapsed during the cataclysmic Sundering War. Seventy years later (70 AE), the land is a patchwork of rival baronies, independent racial settlements, and lawless border zones. Power is held through ownership of land, slaves, workshops, and erotic influence. Slavery is not merely labor — it is the central economic engine, a tool of social control, and a canvas for erotic dominance and submission.
The campaign begins in the Border Barony of Blackthorn Vale, a strategically important but unstable region on the edge of the Shattered Principalities. Its capital, Thornhaven, is a bustling trade hub where cosmopolitan decadence meets brutal pragmatism. Beyond the city walls lie dense forests, rugged hills, and independent racial enclaves where old animosities still simmer.
History & Timeline Context
Before the Sundering War, several grand multi-racial empires controlled the region. The war shattered these empires, leaving behind ruined cities, cursed battlefields, and a generation scarred by loss. The 46 AE famine that followed was catastrophic — mass starvation led to widespread debt bondage contracts that became the foundation of modern multi-generational slavery. Poverty accelerated racial animosity in some areas while forcing uneasy cooperation in others. Seventy years on, the region has stabilized into a tense balance of human-led baronies, re-founded racial kingdoms, and mixed cosmopolitan centers.
Geography — The Shattered Principalities: Blackthorn Vale and Neighboring Baronies (70 AE)
The player characters begin in Blackthorn Vale, a strategically vital barony on the storm-lashed northern coast of the Shattered Principalities. Once part of a greater empire before the Sundering War, Blackthorn Vale is now a tense nexus of trade, slavery, and factional intrigue. The land is defined by fertile river valleys, rugged hills rich in ore and timber, dense eastern forests, and a cold, unforgiving northern ocean that serves as both a highway for merchant fleets and a source of smuggling, piracy, and mysterious wrecks.
Thornhaven – Capital of Blackthorn Vale
The largest and most important settlement in the barony, Thornhaven is a walled port-city built around a central market square and a fortified noble district. Its harbors are constantly busy with slave galleys, pleasure barges, and heavy merchant ships. The city is stratified and cosmopolitan: wealthy nobles and guild masters live in opulent riverfront estates, while the underclass crowds into smog-filled slums and dockside tenements. Key features include the Grand Slave Auction House, the multi-denominational Temple Quarter (where the three major churches compete openly), high-end pleasure houses that double as information brokers, and the bustling Craft Guild Quarter. Thornhaven is the political, economic, and cultural heart of the barony — and the natural starting hub for player ambitions.
Detailed information on Thornhaven can be found in Settlement_Thornhaven.txt.
Major Rivers and Terrain of Blackthorn Vale
Three major rivers — the Blackthorn, the Ironvein, and the Verdant — flow northward from the southern hills into the ocean, creating rich alluvial plains ideal for plantations, water-powered mills, and barge traffic. The western hills are dotted with mines and lumber camps; the eastern forests hide dryad groves and ancient ruins. The northern coastline is rocky with hidden coves perfect for smuggling. Lawless border zones on the southern and eastern edges are frequented by Freeborn League cells and independent racial enclaves (orc holds, elven groves, giant camps).
Neighboring Baronies (Current Game Map)
Blackthorn Vale is effectively land-locked on three sides by four rival/neutral baronies, with the cold Northern Ocean forming its fourth border. This five-baronies cluster is the focused region for early-to-mid campaign play.
Ironvein Barony (West – Coastal)
Capital: Jarick
A heavily industrialized barony dominated by the Hammer & Anvil guild and House Ironroot. Rocky cliffs and deep mines produce iron, coal, and rare metals. Slave labor is brutal and industrial; escape attempts are common but rarely successful. Major trade road connects directly to Thornhaven’s western gate. Tension with Blackthorn Vale is high over mining rights and river tariffs.
Detailed information on Jarick can be found in Settlement_Jarick.txt.
Silken Reach Barony (East – Coastal)
Capital: Moonstone
A wealthy, trade-oriented barony ruled by House Silkveil and strongly influenced by the Silken Collar church. Known for luxurious pleasure estates, silk plantations, and bustling coastal ports. More “civilized” on the surface, but its pleasure houses and auction blocks are among the most expensive (and scandal-prone) in the region. The eastern trade road and shared river access make it Blackthorn Vale’s primary commercial partner — and rival.
Detailed information on Moonstone can be found in Settlement_Moonstone.txt.
Verdant March Barony (South-West – Inland)
Capital: Lariss
Fertile plantation country under strong Verdant Cycle church influence and Dryad alliances. Vast slave-worked fields grow exotic herbs, aphrodisiacs, and luxury crops. The barony is outwardly peaceful but fiercely protective of its groves and bloodlines. Border skirmishes over river water rights and escaped slaves are frequent. Ideal source of high-value agricultural slaves and herbal reagents.
Detailed information on Lariss can be found in Settlement_Lariss.txt.
Bloodthorn Barony (South-East – Inland)
Capital: Gurt
The most volatile and lawless of the four neighbors. Ruled by House Bloodvein and riddled with bandit strongholds, Freeborn League safehouses, and independent orc/giant settlements. Rugged hills and thorny badlands make overland travel dangerous. Blackthorn Vale’s eastern border here is heavily patrolled, yet smuggling and slave raids flow both ways. Many players will first encounter serious opposition or black-market opportunities in Bloodthorn territory.
Detailed information on Gurt can be found in Settlement_Gurt.txt.
The Northern Ocean
A cold, treacherous sea that serves as the ultimate northern boundary. Fog-shrouded coasts hide pirate coves and wrecked ships laden with ancient relics or forbidden cargo. Occasional naval patrols from the baronies attempt to control the coastal waters, but the ocean remains the primary route for long-distance trade — and high-risk smuggling runs that can bypass land borders entirely.
Strategic & Narrative Implications
Trade & Economy: Rivers and coastal roads create natural trade arteries that directly feed into the Economy & Trade System and Vehicle System.
Slavery & Defiance: Each barony has its own flavor of slave treatment, creating reputation and faction consequences when moving slaves across borders.
Factions & Politics: The five baronies form a web of alliances, rivalries, and proxy conflicts perfect for Factions_and_Politics and Reputation systems.
Wilderness & Adventure: Border zones and the eastern forests provide immediate access to Wilderness_Areas content.
Rags-to-Riches Hook: Players start with nothing in Thornhaven and can expand outward through property ownership, trade routes, and political maneuvering into any of the four neighboring baronies.
This expanded geography gives us a compact, playable regional map while leaving room for future expansion beyond these five baronies.
Racial Dynamics — Cosmopolitan vs. Backwards
The region is a mixed bag:

Cosmopolitan Areas (Thornhaven and major trade routes): Races mix relatively freely. Slavery is regulated and often luxurious for high-value slaves. Erotic power is openly displayed in noble courts and pleasure houses. Interracial relationships and mixed households are common, though status still matters.
Backwards Enclaves (remote baronies and borderlands): Racial animosity is raw and often violent. Slavery is harsher, with stronger emphasis on brute force and punishment. Human supremacist attitudes are common, though some enclaves are dominated by a single race (e.g., orc warbands or dryad groves).

Slavery as Economic & Social Backbone
Slavery is legal, widespread, and economically essential. Famine-era debt contracts created multi-generational bondage that remains binding. Slaves are property — bought, sold, gifted, or trained like livestock — but high-value individuals (especially those with exceptional Body traits, pleasant Scent, or useful skills) can achieve relative comfort or even influence. Defiance management, Hygiene/Scent care, and erotic training are critical skills for any owner who wishes to maintain profitability and status.
Erotic Power as Social Currency
Public and private displays of dominance and submission are normalized among the elite. Lavish BDSM ceremonies, “gifting” of trained pleasure slaves, and custom erotic items are standard tools for forging alliances, sealing deals, and demonstrating status. Churches, noble houses, and guilds all participate in or profit from the erotic economy, though their attitudes differ sharply.
Major Powers & Tensions

Noble Houses: Compete through wealth, slaves, crafted luxuries, and erotic diplomacy. Scandals involving poor slave treatment or size-mismatch disasters are potent weapons.
Guilds & Black Market: Hammer & Anvil controls legitimate crafting while rival factions and black market groups (Shadow Forge, Crimson Veil, etc.) undercut them with forgeries and forbidden goods.
Churches: The Silken Collar sanctifies erotic ownership, the Broken Chain condemns it as sin, and the Verdant Cycle seeks natural balance. Their rivalry shapes public morality and legal precedents.
Racial Settlements: Some races have re-founded independent communities after the war, creating zones of uneasy truce or open hostility with human baronies.

Player Role & Tone
Players typically begin as emerging landowners, ambitious crafters, or minor nobles seeking to rise through strategic ownership, erotic mastery, and careful navigation of faction politics. The tone is grimdark: power is intoxicating but fragile, pleasure is laced with risk, and every decision carries consequences for slaves, reputation, and survival.
Key Themes

Ownership as both privilege and burden
The blurred line between pleasure and control
Economic volatility caused by raids, defiance, and scandals
Racial tension and uneasy coexistence
The constant shadow of the Sundering and the famine

This setting provides a rich sandbox for stories of ambition, exploitation, erotic power, and precarious empire-building.
