PREGNANCY_BREEDING_RULES - Version 1.11 (Grimdark Erotic Fantasy, 70 AE)

Core Philosophy
Pregnancy and controlled breeding are high-stakes elements of slavery and ownership in the Shattered Principalities. Because offspring of slaves are legally born into slavery, breeding programs represent a long-term investment in generational labor, pleasure assets, and bloodline value. Breeding is never casual — it is a deliberate, mechanical process fraught with risk, cost, and narrative weight. Successful breeding can create premium slaves or signature bloodlines; failures can cause Defiance spikes, health crises, church scandals, or outright rebellion.

Pregnancy is tracked month-by-month and interacts with every major system: Sex_BDSM scenes, Sexual Corruption, Body traits, Herbalism/Alchemy, Magic rituals, Defiance, Reputation Events, Economy, and Time_System.txt v1.1 (seasonal effects, Monthly Aging Checks, generational aging, and long-term consequences).

ADULT TRAIT & RACIAL MATURITY REQUIREMENT
All pregnancy, breeding, lactation, and related mechanics in this file apply exclusively to adult characters and NPCs only.
- “Adult” is a required trait that must be present on any female slave (or player character) before conception, pregnancy tracking, breeding scenes, or lactation rules can be used.
- Maturity age (when the Adult trait is gained) varies by race and is defined in Races_Reference.txt v1.5:
  - Goblins: 12–14 years (fastest maturation).
  - Orcs and Dryads: 16–18 years.
  - Humans, Gnomes, and Giants: 18–20 years.
  - Elves: 25–30 years; Dwarves: 30–40 years (slowest due to long lifespans).
- Any character or NPC lacking the “Adult” trait cannot become pregnant, participate in breeding scenes, or be subject to lactation mechanics. Any attempt to involve non-adult individuals automatically fails, triggers severe narrative and mechanical consequences (including massive Defiance spikes, church scandals, legal penalties under Crime_Justice_System, faction backlash, or campaign-ending repercussions depending on table tone), and is strictly prohibited.
- Offspring from any successful birth begin life without the Adult trait and only gain it upon reaching their race’s maturity age. This rule ensures all breeding content remains strictly adult-only and is a hard prerequisite across the system.

Fertility & Conception Rules
A fertile female slave (or player character) can become pregnant only during a successful Vaginal or Penile sex/breeding scene.
Resolution: d20 + Vaginal/Penile skill + relevant modifiers vs. DC 15 (base).
Modifiers:
High Sexual Corruption in mother (+1 to +4)
Fertility tonics or herbal aids (Herbalism/Alchemy crafted items): +2 to +5
Magical Alteration ritual (Magic_System): +5 to +10 (backlash risk)
Perfect genital compatibility (1–7 scale match): +2 to +4
Poor Hygiene/Scent or size mismatch: –2 to –6
Conception chance can be intentionally suppressed with birth-control tonics (Alchemy DC 18, cost 15–40 gp per month).

Pregnancy Duration & Monthly Checks
Pregnancy lasts 9 in-game months. At the end of each month the owner rolls a d20 vs. DC 12 for complications.
Success: Pregnancy progresses normally.
Failure by 5 or less: Minor complication (–5 to –10 mother’s Defiance, minor health cost).
Failure by 6+: Major complication (miscarriage risk, permanent injury, or +15–30 Defiance spike for mother).
Modifiers to monthly roll:
Excellent Aftercare and nutrition: +4 to +8
Dedicated pregnancy quarters (Property_Ownership upgrade): +3
High Sexual Corruption in mother: +2 (but increases chance of corrupt offspring)
Overwork or harsh treatment: –4 to –10
Seasonal and aging modifiers from Time_System.txt v1.1: Harsh seasons or visible aging of the mother add –2 to –6; favorable seasons or longevity treatments add +2 to +4.

Birth Mechanics
At the end of month 9, roll d20 + modifiers vs. DC 15 for the birth.
Success: Healthy child born (see Offspring Generation).
Failure: Stillbirth, maternal injury, or death (rare but possible without medical aid).
Modifiers: Midwife slaves with Herbalism skill, crafted birthing restraints, or healing salves can grant advantage or +5. Aging mother or poor estate conditions from Time_System add penalties.

Offspring Generation
Each child born to a slave is automatically a legal slave from birth.
Race: Same as mother (70%) or mixed-race with father (30%). Mixed-race offspring inherit blended Body traits.
Body Traits (1–100 spectrum + 1–7 genital/orifice scale): Inherit 60–80% of mother’s values; remainder randomized or influenced by father. Attractiveness, Scent, and genital compatibility are especially heritable. Apply Time_System aging rules from birth onward.
Starting Defiance: 0–30 (lower if mother had low Defiance and received excellent Aftercare during pregnancy).
Starting Sexual Corruption: 0–20 base; +10 per 20 Corruption points the mother had during pregnancy.
Gender: 50/50 unless magical intervention.
Special Traits: 5–15% chance of inheriting exceptional traits (natural high skill aptitude, unique kinks, or minor magical aptitude). Generational aging and potential hybrid traits are tracked using Time_System v1.1.

Inter-Species Breeding Rules
All eight races (Humans, Elves, Orcs, Dwarves, Gnomes, Dryads, Giants, Goblins) can interbreed, but success rates, complications, and offspring viability vary dramatically based on racial compatibility. Inter-species breeding is a high-risk, high-reward endeavor often pursued by the Verdant Consortium and decadent nobles for exotic or specialized bloodlines. Mixed-race slaves frequently command premium prices or face social stigma depending on the pairing.
Racial Compatibility Chart (applied as modifier to conception roll)
Easy (+4): Human-Human, Human-Elf, Human-Orc, Elf-Dryad, Dwarf-Gnome
Moderate (+2): Human-Dwarf, Human-Gnome, Orc-Dwarf, Elf-Goblin, Dryad-Gnome
Difficult (0): Human-Giant, Orc-Giant, Elf-Giant, Dwarf-Giant, Gnome-Giant
Very Difficult (–4): Goblin-Giant, Dryad-Giant, Orc-Goblin, Elf-Orc
Near-Impossible (–8 or automatic failure without magic): Giant with any small race (Goblin/Gnome) unless high Sexual Corruption or magical aid is used
Additional Modifiers for Inter-Species Conception
Genital compatibility (1–7 scale): Difference of 3+ sizes adds –4 to –8 penalty and increases birth complication risk.
Mother’s Sexual Corruption: +1 per 20 points (helps overcome racial barriers and reduces miscarriage chance).
Father’s Sexual Corruption: +1 per 30 points (minor bonus to trait inheritance quality).
Magical assistance (Alteration ritual): +6 to +12 but adds backlash risk and possible mutation in offspring.
Time_System seasonal effects: Harsh seasons add –2 to –6 to conception and complication rolls.

Offspring from Inter-Species Breeding
Body Traits: Blend of both parents (50–70% from mother, 30–50% from father). Genital/orifice capacity on the 1–7 scale averages the parents’ ranges with a small random variance. Apply Time_System aging from birth.
Viability: Easy pairings have 85–95% healthy birth chance. Difficult pairings drop to 40–60%. Near-impossible pairings are usually stillborn or mutated without magic.
Special Hybrid Traits: 10–25% chance of inheriting unique advantages (e.g., Giant strength + Elf grace, Dryad regeneration + Goblin cunning) or drawbacks (e.g., size mismatch health issues, conflicting racial instincts). Generational aging follows Time_System rules and can be stabilized with potions or rituals.
Social & Legal Status: Mixed-race slaves are often viewed as exotic novelties (premium market value) or abominations (reputation penalties with purist factions like Broken Chain or traditional noble houses). Some baronies require registration of hybrid births.

Hybrid Trait Mechanics
Hybrid traits are the unique mechanical expressions of inter-species bloodlines. They represent blended racial heritage and can be stabilized across generations through selective breeding and bloodline creation rules. Hybrid traits provide powerful advantages but always come with grimdark drawbacks and higher instability.
Hybrid Trait Inheritance & Stabilization
Base Chance: Every inter-species offspring has a 10–25% chance (modified by facility tier, mother’s Sexual Corruption, and magical aid) to inherit one or more hybrid traits.
Stabilization in Bloodlines: When creating a hybrid bloodline, the owner may attempt to lock in a specific hybrid trait each generation (d20 + Administration skill + facility bonus vs. DC 20). Success rate improves with higher-level facilities and repeated generations. Time_System generational aging affects stabilization difficulty.
Trait Slots: A single hybrid slave or bloodline can carry 1–3 hybrid traits (more slots increase instability and Defiance floor).
Instability Penalty: All hybrid bloodlines have a permanent +10 to +25 Defiance floor and +2 to +5 monthly complication DC during pregnancy. Aging accelerates instability in later generations.

Magical Mutation Mechanics
Magical mutations are a constant danger when using Alteration rituals, enchanted breeding facilities, or over-reliance on magic to force conception, accelerate gestation, or enhance traits. Mutations occur primarily from backlash in the Magic_System or from repeated magical interventions during pregnancy/breeding.
Mutation Trigger
Whenever a magical ritual is used for breeding (Alteration for compatibility, acceleration, or trait enhancement) or when an Enchanted/Forbidden Breeding Sanctum is used, roll a separate d20 + (Arcana skill ÷ 5) vs. DC 22.
Natural 1 or failure by 5+: Mutation occurs.
Critical success: Optional beneficial mutation (player choice, GM approval).
Mutation Severity Table (d20)
1–5: Minor Mutation – Cosmetic or low-impact (e.g., unusual eye color, faint magical glow, altered Scent). No mechanical penalty but may affect market value or reputation.
6–12: Moderate Mutation – Noticeable mechanical effect (e.g., +1 to one Body trait but –2 to another; minor skill bonus with drawback). +5–15 permanent Defiance floor for mother and offspring.
13–17: Major Mutation – Significant change (e.g., partial racial shift, new hybrid trait with instability; genital size permanently altered by 1–2 points on 1–7 scale). +15–30 Defiance floor; possible church investigation or reputation loss.
18–20: Cursed Mutation – Severe and often debilitating (e.g., chronic pain, uncontrollable magical backlash during BDSM scenes, permanent infertility, or “monstrous” appearance). +30–50 Defiance floor; high risk of rebellion, low market value, and major faction hostility.
Mutation Categories & Examples
Beneficial / Double-Edged Mutations
Arcane Sensitivity: +3 to Arcana and Erotic Performance rolls involving magic; offspring more likely to inherit magical aptitude. Drawback: +10 Defiance during any ritual use.
Elastic Form: Genital/orifice capacity gains +2 tolerance permanently (stacks with Corruption). Drawback: Higher risk of injury in non-magical scenes.
Neutral / Flavor Mutations
Chromatic Skin: Skin shifts colors subtly with mood or arousal. No mechanical effect but adds exotic market premium or scandal risk.
Echoing Aura: Voice or presence carries faint magical resonance during scenes. +1 to domination rolls but easily detectable by rival mages.
Harmful / Cursed Mutations
Unstable Essence: Monthly random Defiance spike (+10–30). Offspring have higher baseline Defiance.
Necrotic Taint (from Necromancy involvement): “Death scent” permanently lowers pleasant Scent value; –4 to all social/erotic rolls involving close contact. Church condemnation automatic.
Overgrown Limbs or Organs: Extreme size mismatch (genital/orifice shift of +3 or more). Grants intimidation bonuses but causes chronic pain and high injury risk in scenes.
Mutation Propagation
Mutations can be inherited (30–70% chance depending on severity) and stabilized into bloodlines, turning a curse into a rare “cursed bloodline” that some decadent factions prize.
Magical removal of mutations requires another high-risk Alteration ritual (DC 28+, backlash chance doubled).

Risks Specific to Inter-Species Breeding
Dramatically higher monthly complication DCs (+3 to +10 depending on compatibility).
Increased Defiance spikes for the mother (+10–40 during pregnancy).
Higher chance of permanent mutations or infertility in mother or child.
Church & Faction Reactions: Silken Collar and Verdant Consortium reward successful exotic pairings; Broken Chain and Verdant Cycle often condemn them as unnatural. Public hybrid births can trigger major Reputation Events or scandals.

Magical & Alchemical Assistance
Alteration rituals can temporarily adjust genital compatibility or force viable conception (high backlash and mutation risk to both mother and child).
Custom alchemical tonics (multi-skill Herbalism + Alchemy projects) can improve compatibility by 1–2 steps but carry side-effect risks (addiction, permanent Scent/Hygiene changes). Longevity potions can mitigate maternal aging during repeated pregnancies.

Economic & Reputation Value
Successful exotic hybrid slaves can sell for 2×–5× standard prices if they display desirable blended traits.
Failed or visibly mutated hybrids can crash an owner’s reputation or trigger investigations.

Breeding Program Management
Owners may designate “studs” (male slaves or free individuals) for controlled breeding.
Breeding scenes count as specialized Vaginal/Penile scenes and can simultaneously reduce Defiance while attempting conception.
Facilities: Requires upgraded slave quarters with nursery wing (Property_Ownership feature, cost 500+ gp).
Selective Breeding: Over generations, owners can create “signature bloodlines” prized by the Verdant Consortium and noble houses (premium market value 2×–4× normal).

Bloodline Creation Rules
Owners with dedicated breeding programs can selectively breed over multiple generations to create stable “signature bloodlines.” A bloodline is a tracked lineage of slaves sharing consistent, improved traits. Creating a bloodline takes 2–5 generations (20–50+ in-game years) and requires consistent investment in facilities, care, and selective pairing. Time_System generational aging and longevity potions heavily influence long-term success.

Bloodline Creation Process
Establish Foundation (Generation 0): Select 4–8 high-quality breeding slaves with desirable starting traits (high Attractiveness, favorable 1–7 genital/orifice compatibility, low Defiance, high Sexual Corruption).
Selective Pairing: Each generation, choose specific studs and dams based on inherited traits. Roll d20 + Administration skill + facility bonus vs. DC 18 to “stabilize” a desired trait. Aging and seasonal effects modify stabilization difficulty.
Trait Stabilization: Each successful generation has a 60–80% chance (modified by facility tier and care) to lock in or improve one chosen trait (e.g., +10 Attractiveness, +1 to genital size range, unique kink affinity).
Bloodline Level: Track bloodline quality from 1 (basic) to 5 (legendary). Higher levels require more generations and better facilities.
Level 1–2: Minor bonuses (+1–2 to Body traits, 5–10% higher market value).
Level 3–4: Significant bonuses (+3–5 to traits, signature kink or skill aptitude, 2×–3× market value).
Level 5: Legendary bloodline (exceptional traits, possible minor magical affinity, 4×+ market value, reputation boosts with decadent factions).
